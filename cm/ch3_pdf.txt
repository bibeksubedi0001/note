Economic Order Quantity (EOQ)
Definition: 
´âİIt is a technique of inventory management. 
´âİIt determines the optimum order size that will minimize the total inventory cost. 
´âİIt is a  recorder quantity which explains how much quantity has to be ordered at a 
time to optimize the cost of carrying and holding inventory.  
´âİEOQ  is a measurement used in the field of operations, logistics, and supply 
management. 
´âİIn principle, EOQ is a tool used to determine the volume and frequency of orders 
required to satisfy a given level of demand while minimizing the cost per order.
´âİIt is a set point designed to help companies minimize the ordering and holding 
inventory.
´âİThe cost of ordering inventory falls with the increase on ordering volume due to 
purchasing on economics of scale.
´âİAs the size of inventory grows, the cost of holding the inventory rises.
´âİIt is the exact point that minimizes both of these inversely related cost.

´âİSafety Stock: Floating stock (Minimum stock)

account of minimum level, lead time and normal demand. It prevents 
stock-outs.

3.6 Application of Value Engineering in the Procurement of Materials
Value Engineering: 
´âİAn analysis of materials, processes, and products in which functions are related 
to cost and from which a selection may be made so as achieve the desired 
function at the lowest overall cost consistent with performance.
´âİAn organized, creative, cost search technique for analyzing the function of a 
product with the purpose of value enhancement without compromising quality, 
performance & efficiency.  
Value= Worth / Cost
´éğValue means reliable performance 
´éğFunction / Worth means natural action performed
´éğCost means expenditure

Value Analysis & Value Engineering
´âİValue analysis & Value engineering is defined as an organized approach to 
provide the required function at the lowest cost consistent with specific 
performance, quality & reliability.
´âİValue analysis refers to the existing product & services where as value 
engineering is concerned to the design of new Products.
Use of Value Engineering:
´âİIt is used to determine the best design alternatives for projects, processes, 
products, or services
´âİIt is used to reduce cost
´âİIt is used to improve quality, increase reliability & availability, and 
customer satisfaction 
´âİIt is also used to improve organizational performance
´âİIt is powerful tool used to identify problems and develop recommended 
solution

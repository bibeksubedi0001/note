EOQ Technique Summary

Economic Order Quantity (EOQ) Formulas

EOQ Formula

$$\text{EOQ} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times D \times C_o}{C_i \times P}}$$

Where: $D$ = Annual demand, $C_o$ = Ordering cost per order, $C_i$ = Carrying cost rate (fraction), $P$ = Unit price

Related Formulas

$$\text{Number of Orders} = \frac{D}{\text{EOQ}}$$

$$\text{Cycle Time} = \frac{\text{EOQ}}{d} \quad (d = \text{daily consumption} = D / \text{working days})$$

$$\text{ROL} = (\text{Lead Time} \times d) + \text{Safety Stock}$$

$$\text{Max Stock} = \text{EOQ} + \text{Safety Stock}$$

$$\text{Min Stock} = \text{Safety Stock}$$

$$\text{Average Stock} = \frac{\text{EOQ}}{2} + \text{Safety Stock}$$

$$\text{GiT (Goods in Transit)} = \text{Lead Time} \times d$$

$$\text{Total Inventory Cost} = \frac{D}{\text{EOQ}} \times C_o + \frac{\text{EOQ}}{2} \times C_i \times P$$

ABC Classification Technique

ABC Analysis Procedure

Steps
  1. Calculate Annual Usage Value = Quantity × Rate per unit for each material
  2. Rank materials in descending order of usage value
  3. Calculate cumulative percentage of total value
  4. Classify:
    • A: Top 70–80% of value (usually ~10–20% of items) — High control
    • B: Next 15–20% of value (~20–30% of items) — Medium control
    • C: Remaining 5–10% of value (~50–70% of items) — Low control

Q2. EOQ & Inventory 2079 Jestha

A construction site of residential building with large floor area requires 6000 bags of cement in a year where a material supplier promised to supply within the five working days after placing the order. If site requires storage area separately which costs 15% storing charge per unit per year at that locality? Construction work will be carried for 200 days in a year and site engineer is planning for ordering the quantity of cement at a time by considering ordering cost per order of Rs 25000 charged by material supplier. What quantity can be suggested as EOQ. Is there any goods in transit (GiT)? If yes, calculate GiT too. What would be the re-order level, if site engineer wants to minimum 190 bags of cement as minimum safety stock? [6 marks]

Solution

Given

D = 6,000 bags/year, LT = 5 days, Ci = 15% = 0.15, Working days = 200, Co = Rs.25,000

Safety Stock (SS) = 190 bags

Note: Unit price P is not given directly. For cement, we can assume a reasonable rate. However, EOQ formula without P uses: EOQ = √(2DCo/Ch) where Ch = carrying cost per unit per year = Ci × P.

Since P isn't given, we use the carrying cost per unit per year. Assuming cement rate ≈ Rs.750/bag (common rate):

Ch = 0.15 × 750 = Rs.112.5/bag/year

EOQ Calculation

$$\text{EOQ} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 6000 \times 25000}{112.5}}$$

$$= \sqrt{\frac{300{,}000{,}000}{112.5}} = \sqrt{2{,}666{,}667}$$

$$\boxed{\text{EOQ} \approx 1{,}633 \text{ bags}}$$

Daily Consumption

$$d = \frac{D}{\text{Working days}} = \frac{6000}{200} = 30 \text{ bags/day}$$

Goods in Transit (GiT)

$$\text{GiT} = \text{Lead Time} \times d = 5 \times 30 = \textbf{150 bags}$$

Yes, there are goods in transit = 150 bags

Reorder Level (ROL)

$$\text{ROL} = (\text{LT} \times d) + \text{SS} = (5 \times 30) + 190 = 150 + 190 = \textbf{340 bags}$$

★ EOQ ≈ 1,633 bags
★ GiT = 150 bags (Yes, goods in transit exist)
★ Re-order Level = 340 bags

Q3. EOQ & Stock Levels 2078 Kartik

Calculate EOQ, Maximum Stock level, minimum, average stock level, and ROP/ROL if annual cement required for a construction project is 20000 bags; ordering cost is Rs. 10,000; Carrying cost is 5%; Cement rate is Rs. 900 per bags; Safety stock is 10 days; lead time is 5 days. Also calculate the total inventory cost. [6 marks]

Solution

Given

D = 20,000 bags/year, Co = Rs.10,000, Ci = 5% = 0.05, P = Rs.900/bag, LT = 5 days

Assuming 365 working days/year: d = 20,000/365 = 54.79 bags/day

Safety Stock = 10 days × 54.79 = 548 bags

EOQ

$$C_h = C_i \times P = 0.05 \times 900 = \text{Rs.}45\text{/bag/year}$$

$$\text{EOQ} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 20{,}000 \times 10{,}000}{45}}$$

$$= \sqrt{\frac{400{,}000{,}000}{45}} = \sqrt{8{,}888{,}889}$$

$$\boxed{\text{EOQ} \approx 2{,}981 \text{ bags}}$$

Stock Levels

$$\text{Max Stock} = \text{EOQ} + \text{SS} = 2{,}981 + 548 = \textbf{3,529 bags}$$

$$\text{Min Stock} = \text{SS} = \textbf{548 bags}$$

$$\text{Avg Stock} = \frac{\text{EOQ}}{2} + \text{SS} = 1{,}490.5 + 548 = \textbf{2,039 bags}$$

Reorder Point (ROP/ROL)

$$\text{ROL} = (\text{LT} \times d) + \text{SS} = (5 \times 54.79) + 548 = 274 + 548 = \textbf{822 bags}$$

Total Inventory Cost

$$\text{Ordering Cost} = \frac{D}{\text{EOQ}} \times C_o = \frac{20{,}000}{2{,}981} \times 10{,}000 = \text{Rs.}67{,}100$$

$$\text{Carrying Cost} = \frac{\text{EOQ}}{2} \times C_h = \frac{2{,}981}{2} \times 45 = \text{Rs.}67{,}073$$

$$\boxed{\text{Total Inventory Cost} = 67{,}100 + 67{,}073 = \text{Rs.}1{,}34{,}173}$$

(Note: At EOQ, ordering cost ≈ carrying cost, which verifies our EOQ calculation.)

★ EOQ ≈ 2,981 bags
★ Max Stock = 3,529 bags | Min Stock = 548 bags | Avg Stock = 2,039 bags
★ ROL = 822 bags
★ Total Inventory Cost = Rs.1,34,173

Q4. EOQ & Reorder Point 2071 Bhadra

Calculate EOQ and reorder point if rebar required is 1000 tonnes; Ordering Cost is Rs.10,000; Carrying Cost is 15%, rebar rate is Rs.90,000 per tonne; Safety stock is 50 tonnes; lead time is 4 day and consumption per day is 50 tonnes. [Part of 6 marks]

Solution

Given

D = 1,000 tonnes, Co = Rs.10,000, Ci = 15% = 0.15, P = Rs.90,000/tonne

SS = 50 tonnes, LT = 4 days, d = 50 tonnes/day

EOQ

$$C_h = C_i \times P = 0.15 \times 90{,}000 = \text{Rs.}13{,}500\text{/tonne/year}$$

$$\text{EOQ} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 1{,}000 \times 10{,}000}{13{,}500}}$$

$$= \sqrt{\frac{20{,}000{,}000}{13{,}500}} = \sqrt{1{,}481.48}$$

$$\boxed{\text{EOQ} \approx 38.49 \approx 39 \text{ tonnes}}$$

Reorder Point

$$\text{ROL} = (\text{LT} \times d) + \text{SS} = (4 \times 50) + 50 = 200 + 50 = \textbf{250 tonnes}$$

★ EOQ ≈ 39 tonnes
★ Reorder Point = 250 tonnes

Q5. Simple EOQ 2070 Magh

Calculate Economic Order Quantity of Cement with following details; Total Quantity = 12000 bags, Ci=15%, Co=10000, P = 750 Rs. per bag. [3 marks]

Solution

Given

D = 12,000 bags, Ci = 15% = 0.15, Co = Rs.10,000, P = Rs.750/bag

EOQ Calculation

$$C_h = C_i \times P = 0.15 \times 750 = \text{Rs.}112.5\text{/bag/year}$$

$$\text{EOQ} = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times D \times C_o}{C_h}}$$

$$= \sqrt{\frac{2 \times 12{,}000 \times 10{,}000}{112.5}}$$

$$= \sqrt{\frac{240{,}000{,}000}{112.5}} = \sqrt{2{,}133{,}333}$$

$$\boxed{\text{EOQ} \approx 1{,}461 \text{ bags}}$$

★ EOQ ≈ 1,461 bags

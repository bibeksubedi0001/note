Valuation of Properties

18 Solved Problems 2070–2081

Valuation Formulas & Methods

Year’s Purchase (YP) with Sinking Fund
$$\text{YP} = \frac{1}{i + s}, \qquad s = \frac{r}{(1+r)^n - 1}$$ $i$ = net yield rate,   $r$ = sinking fund interest rate,   $n$ = future life (years)

Capitalized Value Method
$$\text{Capitalized Value} = \text{Net Annual Income} \times \text{YP} + \text{Cost of Land}$$ $$\text{Net Income} = \text{Gross Rent} - \text{Total Outgoings}$$ Plinth Area Method
$$\text{Building Value} = \text{Plinth Area} \times \text{Rate/m}^2 - \text{Depreciation}$$ $$\text{Dep. (SLM)} = \frac{\text{Cost} - \text{Scrap Value}}{\text{Total Life}} \times \text{Age}$$ Development Method
$$\text{Land Value} = \text{Total Sale Price} - \text{Development Costs} - \text{Profit}$$ Mortgage Value   $= \tfrac{2}{3} \times \text{Capitalized Value}$

Sinking Fund
$$A = \frac{(C - S) \cdot r}{(1+r)^n - 1}$$

Q1. Development Method 2081 Development

A prospective investor desires to purchase a piece of converted land measuring 1,56,000 sqft in area. After developing this land for housing colony, he wants to sell the plots (1700 sqft in area) at the rate of Rs 9300 per sqft which is prevailing in the vicinity and thus earn net profit of 22% in the bargain. With the following information, work out the valuation of the undeveloped property. Express the value of property per sqft basis. [7]

a) Deduction for roads, parks, electric sub-stations = 30% area
b) Levelling, dressing and jungle clearance cost = Rs 5 per sqft
c) Cost of providing roads, drainage, w/s and electric sub-station = Rs 400 per sqft
d) Engineer’s fee and miscellaneous charges = 5% of sale price
e) Legal charges, brokerage and advertisement = 10% on sale price

Solution

Step 1 — Saleable Area
Deduction = 30% of 1,56,000 = 46,800 sqft
$\text{Saleable area} = 1{,}56{,}000 - 46{,}800 = 1{,}09{,}200 \text{ sqft}$
Step 2 — Total Sale Price
$\text{SP} = 1{,}09{,}200 \times 9{,}300 = \text{Rs.}\,1{,}01{,}55{,}60{,}000$
Step 3 — Development Costs
ItemCalculationAmount (Rs.)
Levelling & clearance1,56,000 × 57,80,000
Roads, drainage, W/S, substation1,56,000 × 4006,24,00,000
Engineer’s fee (5% of SP)0.05 × SP5,07,78,000
Legal, brokerage, ads (10% of SP)0.10 × SP10,15,56,000
Total Development Cost21,55,14,000
Step 4 — Profit
$\text{Profit} = 22\% \times 1{,}01{,}55{,}60{,}000 = \text{Rs.}\,22{,}34{,}23{,}200$
Step 5 — Land Value
$$\text{Value} = \text{SP} - \text{Dev. Cost} - \text{Profit} = 1{,}01{,}55{,}60{,}000 - 21{,}55{,}14{,}000 - 22{,}34{,}23{,}200$$ $$= \text{Rs.}\,57{,}66{,}22{,}800$$ $\text{Per sqft} = 57{,}66{,}22{,}800 \div 1{,}56{,}000 = \text{Rs.}\,3{,}696.30$
▶ Value of Undeveloped Land = Rs.57,66,22,800  |  Rs.3,696.30/sqft

Q2. Plinth Area Method 2080 Plinth Area

A freehold property consisting of RCC building having a total area of 500 m² constructed 10 years ago is proposed to be purchased by Mr. Thapa. The present market rate of construction for similar type of building is Rs 45,000/m². The total area of land is 500 m². The commercial market rate (CMR) of this land is Rs 1,00,000/m². The government registration rate (GRR) for this land is Rs 30,000/m². Use 60:40 proportion for determining cost of land. Assume life of building to be 70 years and scrap value as 10%. Advise Mr. Thapa as to what cost he should purchase the property. Use Plinth area method of valuation and straight line method of depreciation. [6]

Solution

Step 1 — Cost of Land
Using 60:40 proportion (CMR : GRR):
$\text{Rate} = 0.60 \times 1{,}00{,}000 + 0.40 \times 30{,}000 = \text{Rs.}\,72{,}000/\text{m}^2$
$\text{Land} = 500 \times 72{,}000 = \text{Rs.}\,3{,}60{,}00{,}000$
Step 2 — Building Cost
$\text{Building} = 500 \times 45{,}000 = \text{Rs.}\,2{,}25{,}00{,}000$
Step 3 — Depreciation (SLM)
$\text{Scrap} = 10\% = \text{Rs.}\,22{,}50{,}000$ $$\text{Dep.} = \frac{2{,}25{,}00{,}000 - 22{,}50{,}000}{70} \times 10 = 2{,}89{,}286 \times 10 = \text{Rs.}\,28{,}92{,}857$$
Step 4 — Depreciated Value
$\text{Building} = 2{,}25{,}00{,}000 - 28{,}92{,}857 = \text{Rs.}\,1{,}96{,}07{,}143$
Step 5 — Total Value
$\text{Total} = 3{,}60{,}00{,}000 + 1{,}96{,}07{,}143 = \text{Rs.}\,5{,}56{,}07{,}143$
▶ Mr. Thapa should purchase at approximately Rs.5,56,07,143

Q6. Capitalized Value 2078 Ch. Capitalized

A building, having estimated future life of 80 years, fetches a gross annual rent of Rs. 2500 per month. Work out its capitalized value on the basis of 8% net yield. The rate of above building measures 1200 m² and cost of land is taken at Rs. 100 per m². The other outgoing are:
Repair and maintenance (1/12)th of gross income
Municipal axis and property tax – 25% of gross income
Management and miscellaneous charges – 8% of gross income
The plinth area of a building is 600 m² and plinth area rate of the building is Rs. 200 per m². [6]

Solution

Step 1 — Gross Annual Income
The building fetches a gross rent of Rs. 2,500 per month.
Gross Annual Rent = $2{,}500 \times 12 = \text{Rs.}\,30{,}000$
Step 2 — Calculation of Outgoings
Repair and maintenance (1/12th of gross income) = $30{,}000 \times \frac{1}{12} = \text{Rs.}\,2{,}500$
Municipal and property tax (25% of gross income) = $30{,}000 \times 0.25 = \text{Rs.}\,7{,}500$
Management and miscellaneous (8% of gross income) = $30{,}000 \times 0.08 = \text{Rs.}\,2{,}400$

Sinking Fund Calculation:
Cost of building = Plinth Area × Plinth Area Rate = $600 \times 200 = \text{Rs.}\,1{,}20{,}000$
(Note: Land cost = $1{,}200 \times 100 = \text{Rs.}\,1{,}20{,}000$ — land does not depreciate, so not included in sinking fund)
Scrap value of building (assume 10%) = $1{,}20{,}000 \times 0.10 = \text{Rs.}\,12{,}000$
Amount to be accumulated (S) = $1{,}20{,}000 - 12{,}000 = \text{Rs.}\,1{,}08{,}000$
Assuming SF interest rate = 4% (0.04):
$$I = \frac{S \times i}{(1+i)^n - 1} = \frac{1{,}08{,}000 \times 0.04}{(1.04)^{80} - 1} = \frac{4{,}320}{23.0498 - 1} = \frac{4{,}320}{22.0498} \approx \text{Rs.}\,195.92$$

Total Outgoings = R&M + Tax + Management + Sinking Fund
= $2{,}500 + 7{,}500 + 2{,}400 + 195.92 = \text{Rs.}\,12{,}595.92$
Step 3 — Net Annual Income
Net Income = Gross Annual Income − Total Outgoings
Net Income = $30{,}000 - 12{,}595.92 = \text{Rs.}\,17{,}404.08$
Step 4 — Capitalized Value
Net yield = 8%
Year’s Purchase (YP) = $\frac{100}{\text{Rate of Interest}} = \frac{100}{8} = 12.5$

Capitalized Value = Net Income × YP
Capitalized Value = $17{,}404.08 \times 12.5 = \text{Rs.}\,2{,}17{,}551$
▶ Capitalized Value of the property = Rs. 2,17,551

A building fetches a gross rent of Rs. 20,000.00 per month. Assuming 25% outgoings for taxes, 20% for repairs and maintenance and 5% to set aside to accumulate the cost of building. Determine its capitalized value at the rate of interest on capital as 6% per annum and 4% on sinking fund installment. [5]

Solution

Net Income
Gross = $20{,}000 \times 12 = \text{Rs.}\,2{,}40{,}000$
Total outgoings = $(25 + 20 + 5)\% = 50\%$ = Rs.1,20,000
$\text{Net} = 2{,}40{,}000 - 1{,}20{,}000 = \text{Rs.}\,1{,}20{,}000$

Q18. Sinking Fund — Useful Life 2072 Sinking Fund

The annual sinking fund of concrete vibrating machine costing Rs.50,000 is Rs. 150 and its salvage value is estimated to be Rs.5,000 only. Assuming interest rate as 4%. Determine the useful life of the machine. [4]

Solution

Setup
From $A = \dfrac{(C - S) \cdot r}{(1+r)^n - 1}$, substituting values: $$150 = \frac{(50{,}000 - 5{,}000) \times 0.04}{(1.04)^n - 1} = \frac{1{,}800}{(1.04)^n - 1}$$
Solve for $n$
$$(1.04)^n - 1 = \frac{1{,}800}{150} = 12 \implies (1.04)^n = 13$$ $$n = \frac{\ln(13)}{\ln(1.04)} = \frac{2.5649}{0.03922} = 65.4 \text{ years}$$
▶ Useful Life of Machine ≈ 65 years

  8. Write short notes on: Responsibilities of Site Engineer. [3] (2070 Bhadra)
  • Site engineers perform a technical, organizational, and supervisory role on construction projects
  • Setting out and determining above and underground infrastructural installations
  • Exercise their duty of care to all persons lawfully on site
  • Work as part of the site management team liaising alongside architects, engineers, construction managers, planners, surveyors, and subcontractors

Roles and Responsibilities 2081 2079 2077 2074 2072 2070 2069

  • Studying the work plan submitted by the contractor and suggesting modifications
  • Watching and inspecting the construction work to ensure full accordance with drawings, specifications, and bills of quantities
  • Supervising works on-site in accordance with contract documents and using templates and procedures of the consultant
  • Inspecting and testing materials prior to their use as per sample approved by consultant; ensuring rejected material is removed from site
  • Ensuring correct implementation of the specifications, designs, and quality requirements
  • Checking of layout and setting out of buildings, structures, and site levels
  • Preparing a list of tests required and tests to be carried out at the site; keeping a log of all tests
  • Checking and testing completed works before they are covered; taking photos on a regular basis and issuing instructions on account of defective work
  • Checking and ensuring that the site is arranged per the site management plan and that all measures are taken for site safety and the safety of third parties
  • Ensuring that health and safety rules are adopted and followed to the full extent
  • Preparing weekly reports and suggesting remedial measures if needed
  • Preparation of the list of critical items and their close monitoring
  • Maintaining a filing system for all site memos, instructions, measured quantities of work and materials, and other documents related to construction activities
  • Maintaining a site order book to be made available for consultants and senior officers to write comments or defects in construction

Required Qualities of a Site Engineer

  • Knowledge of technology
  • Social understanding
  • Economical realities
  • Legal awareness
  • Environmental skills
  • Management
  • Leadership and innovation

Administrative Functions

  • Required from time to time by administrative head/consultants
  • Interacting with local authorities/departments for approval/certificates as instructed
  • Performing other relevant duties as assigned

Deliverables

  • Weekly progress reports per each project
  • Compliance of comments on defective work
  • Site order book
  • Update of the work plan of the contractor
  • Duly maintained electronic library of the works

Monitoring and Progress Controls

  • Monitoring daily progress of the site
  • Preparing progress report to be submitted to consultants
  • Keeping track of work in line with the contract

  4. Explain: Site Order Book. [1] (2079 Jestha)
  5. What is site order book? [2] (2072 Magh)
  6. Explain site order book. [2] (2071 Magh)
  • A site order book is a register maintained in the name of the project
  • It is a certified book showing the number of pages it contains
  • Each page is numbered and no page can be removed under any circumstances
  • The Engineer or his authorized representative shall write instructions regarding any work which needs action by the contractor (e.g., improvement in quality, performance adhering to schedule, etc.)
  • The contractor shall promptly sign and acknowledge the orders given by the Engineer-In-Charge and comply with them
  • The contractor shall make the site order book available to the Engineer or representative in time so that it can be checked

  • The entire finance is based on this record — so the record should be lucid, clear, and accurate
  • It contains details so that the entries made can be checked with the measurement
  • The total quantity acts as a valid document

  • Description of works
  • Drawing number
  • Detailed measurement entries

Key Exam Patterns

  • Site Engineer responsibilities: Asked in almost every exam — list at least 8-10 key responsibilities
  • Format questions: "Prepare format of Site Order Book" and "Prepare muster roll format" — know the table columns
  • Differentiation questions: Running Bill vs Final Bill, Site Order Book vs Measurement Book — prepare comparison tables
  • Short notes pattern: MB, Muster Roll, SOB, Record Keeping frequently asked as 2-3 mark short notes

Must-Know Points

  1. Site engineer roles: Quality control, measurement, record keeping, coordination, safety, progress monitoring, material verification, compliance with specifications
  2. Site Order Book: Written instructions from engineer to contractor; legally binding; includes date, instruction, signature
  3. Measurement Book: Official record of all measurements taken at site; basis for payment; maintained by engineer
  4. Muster Roll columns: Name, sex, date of joining, roll number, employment type, category, payment rate, shift, attendance
  5. Running Bill: Intermediate payment based on work done; Final Bill: complete payment after project completion with all adjustments
  6. Bill preparation procedure: Measurement → MB entry → Abstract → Bill preparation → Check → Certification → Payment
  7. Record keeping documents: SOB, MB, Muster Roll, Log Book, Hindrance Register, Material at Site (MAS) register

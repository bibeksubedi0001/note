Construction Safety

Construction is one of the most hazardous industries. Safety management aims to prevent accidents, injuries, and fatalities at construction sites.

Common Types of Accidents

  • Falls from height: Scaffolding, roofs, ladders, open edges — the leading cause of death
  • Struck by objects: Falling materials, swinging loads, flying debris
  • Caught in/between: Machinery, cave-ins, collapsing structures
  • Electrocution: Contact with power lines, faulty wiring
  • Vehicle/equipment accidents: Rollovers, collisions, run-overs
  • Excavation collapse: Trench cave-ins without proper shoring

Causes of Accidents

  • Unsafe acts: Not wearing PPE, improper use of tools, shortcuts, horseplay
  • Unsafe conditions: Defective equipment, poor housekeeping, inadequate lighting, unguarded openings
  • Lack of training: Workers unaware of hazards and safe procedures
  • Poor supervision: Inadequate monitoring and enforcement of safety rules
  • Management failure: No safety policy, inadequate budget for safety

Safety Measures

  • Personal Protective Equipment (PPE): Helmet, safety boots, gloves, goggles, harness, high-visibility vest
  • Safety barriers and guards: Guardrails, toe boards, safety nets around open edges
  • Proper scaffolding: Standard scaffolding with planks, guardrails, access ladders
  • Excavation safety: Shoring, benching, sloping for trench stability
  • Safety training: Induction training, toolbox talks, emergency drills
  • First aid: First aid kits, trained first-aiders at site
  • Signage: Warning signs, restricted area markers, safety instructions
  • Safety officer: Dedicated safety officer for medium-large projects
  • Safety committee: Joint management-worker safety committee

Definition

Workman’s Compensation is the legal provision requiring employers to provide financial compensation to workers who suffer injuries, disabilities, or death arising out of and during the course of employment.

Key Provisions (Nepal Labor Act)

  • Employer is liable to pay compensation regardless of fault (no-fault liability)
  • Covers all types of injuries, occupational diseases, and death at work
  • Compensation amount varies based on severity: temporary disability, permanent partial disability, permanent total disability, death

Types of Compensation

TypeCoverage
Temporary DisabilityMedical expenses + wages during recovery period
Permanent Partial DisabilityLump sum based on percentage of disability
Permanent Total DisabilityFull compensation (100% disability)
DeathCompensation to dependents/family
Medical ExpensesAll reasonable medical treatment costs

Employer’s Obligations

  • Maintain insurance for workers’ compensation
  • Report accidents to relevant authorities
  • Provide first aid and medical treatment
  • Pay wages during disability period
  • Maintain safe working conditions

Fire Regulations

  • Fire safety plan required for all construction sites
  • Fire extinguishers at accessible locations (CO2 for electrical, dry powder for general, foam for liquids)
  • Fire exits and escape routes clearly marked
  • No smoking in designated areas (near fuel, chemicals, timber stores)
  • Hot work permits for welding, cutting, etc.
  • Fire drills and training for workers
  • Storage of flammable materials in designated areas with proper ventilation

Insurance in Construction

  • Workman’s Compensation Insurance: Covers worker injuries and death
  • Third-Party Liability Insurance: Covers damage to third party (people, property) caused by construction activities
  • Fire Insurance: Covers fire damage to structures, materials, equipment
  • Equipment Insurance: Covers damage/loss of construction equipment
  • Professional Liability Insurance: For consultants/designers against design errors

Contractor’s All Risk (CAR) Insurance

CAR Policy is a comprehensive insurance that covers all risks associated with a construction project in a single policy.

Coverage

  • Material damage to the works under construction
  • Materials and equipment at site
  • Third-party liability
  • Loss or damage due to fire, flood, earthquake, storm
  • Theft and vandalism
  • Defective design, material, or workmanship

Exclusions (Common)

  • Wear and tear, gradual deterioration
  • Consequential loss (loss of profit, contract penalties)
  • War and nuclear risks
  • Willful negligence

Environmental Impact of Construction

  • Air pollution (dust, emissions from equipment)
  • Noise pollution (equipment, blasting)
  • Water pollution (sediment runoff, chemical spills)
  • Land degradation (excavation, deforestation)
  • Waste generation (construction and demolition waste)
  • Habitat destruction

Environmental Protection Act (EPA) 1997 — Nepal

  • Requires Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA) for large projects
  • Requires Initial Environmental Examination (IEE) for medium projects
  • EIA must be approved before project implementation
  • Includes public hearing and stakeholder consultation
  • Environmental monitoring during construction required

Environmental Mitigation Measures

  • Dust suppression (water spraying, covering stockpiles)
  • Noise barriers and restricted working hours
  • Sedimentation ponds and erosion control
  • Proper waste management and disposal
  • Tree plantation and habitat restoration
  • Use of environment-friendly materials and practices

Nepal National Building Code (NBC)

A set of minimum standards for the design and construction of buildings in Nepal. Four levels:

LevelDescriptionApplication
NBC 000Requirements for State-of-the-Art DesignAll professionally designed buildings
NBC 100Requirements for Engineered StructuresBuildings designed by engineers
NBC 200Requirements for Non-Engineered StructuresBuildings without formal engineering design
NBC 300Requirements for Buildings of Restricted SizeSmall residential buildings

Building Bye-Laws

Local regulations that govern building construction in municipalities. They specify:

  • Building setbacks (front, side, rear)
  • Maximum ground coverage and floor area ratio (FAR)
  • Maximum building height
  • Minimum room sizes, ventilation, and natural light
  • Parking requirements
  • Fire safety provisions
  • Building permit procedures
  • Structural safety requirements

Building Permit Process

  1. Submit application with drawings, structural design, ownership documents
  2. Municipality reviews for compliance with bye-laws
  3. Building permit issued (or rejected with reasons)
  4. Construction as per approved drawings
  5. Inspections during construction
  6. Completion certificate issued after final inspection

1 Remote Sensing Basics

  • Definition: Acquiring information about Earth's surface without physical contact, using EM energy.
  • Active RS: Own energy source (RADAR, LiDAR). Day/night, penetrates clouds.
  • Passive RS: Depends on sunlight (Landsat, SPOT). Affected by clouds/darkness.

2 EM Spectrum Bands

  • Visible: Blue (0.45–0.52), Green (0.52–0.60), Red (0.63–0.69 μm).
  • NIR: 0.76–0.90 μm — vegetation health.
  • SWIR: 1.55–2.35 μm — soil moisture, minerals.
  • Thermal IR: 10.4–12.5 μm — surface temperature.
  • Microwave: >1 mm — RADAR, penetrates clouds.

3 Spectral Signatures (MOST ASKED)

Draw this diagram in every related exam question. Spectral signatures are the most frequently tested topic in this chapter.
  • Vegetation: Low visible (photosynthesis absorption), sharp rise at Red Edge (~0.7 μm), very high NIR, dips at water bands (1.4, 1.9 μm).
  • Water: High blue-green, near-zero after red. Clear water absorbs NIR.
  • Soil: Gradual increase visible to SWIR. Wet soil = lower reflectance.

7 NDVI

NDVI = (NIR − Red) / (NIR + Red)
<0 water | 0–0.1 bare | 0.2–0.4 sparse | 0.5–0.8 dense vegetation

8 Change Detection

  • Compare two images from different dates.
  • Methods: image differencing, ratioing, post-classification comparison, PCA.
  • Applications: deforestation, urban growth, flood mapping, glacial retreat.
Q Past Exam Questions
+Draw and explain spectral signatures of vegetation, water, and soil. (Every exam 2071–2082)

Draw reflectance vs wavelength (0.4–2.5 μm). Three curves. Label Red Edge, water absorption bands. Explain each curve shape.

+Explain the four types of resolution in Remote Sensing. (2072, 2074, 2076, 2079, 2081)

Spatial (pixel size), Spectral (bands), Radiometric (bit depth), Temporal (revisit). Give satellite examples.

+Compare active and passive remote sensing. (2071, 2073, 2078)

Active: own energy, day/night, RADAR/LiDAR. Passive: sunlight, limited by clouds, Landsat/SPOT. Draw comparison diagram.

+What are band combinations? Explain True Colour, FCC, SWIR for Landsat 8. (2074, 2076, 2079, 2080, 2082)

True: 4,3,2. FCC: 5,4,3. SWIR: 7,5,4. Include band table. Explain what appears red/green in each.

+Compare supervised and unsupervised classification. (2073, 2075, 2076, 2079, 2081)

Supervised: training data, MLC. Unsupervised: K-Means, ISODATA, user labels after. Use comparison table.

+How is NDVI used for vegetation analysis? (2074 Bhadra, 2075, 2080 Chaitra)

Formula, range. Applications: crop health, forestry, drought. Multi-temporal for seasonal change.

+Explain change detection using RS imagery. (2078, 2080, 2082)

Multi-date images. Pre-processing: geometric + radiometric correction. Methods: differencing, NDVI diff, post-classification. Nepal examples: flood, deforestation.

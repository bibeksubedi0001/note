1 Map Projections

  • Purpose: Transform 3D Earth onto 2D surface. All projections introduce distortion.
  • Types by surface: Cylindrical (Mercator), Conical (Lambert), Azimuthal/Planar (Stereographic).
  • Properties preserved: Conformal (shape), Equal-area (area), Equidistant (distance). No projection preserves all.
  • UTM (Universal Transverse Mercator): 60 zones, each 6 degrees wide, central meridian scale 0.9996.

2 Nepal Datum and MUTM

  • Nepal datum: Nagarkot as origin, Everest 1830 ellipsoid.
  • MUTM (Modified UTM): Zone width reduced to 3 degrees (84–87°E and 87–90°E). Scale factor = 0.9999. More accurate for Nepal's narrow east-west extent.

4 RMS Error

RMS = √[(Σ(xi′ − xi)² + (yi′ − yi)²) / n]

Used in georeferencing to measure accuracy of transformation. Lower RMS = better fit. Acceptable threshold depends on scale (typically <1 pixel).

5 Accuracy vs Precision

  • Accuracy: Closeness to true value. High accuracy = low systematic error.
  • Precision: Repeatability/consistency. High precision = low random error.
  • A measurement can be precise but inaccurate (consistently wrong), or accurate but imprecise (close on average but scattered).

6 Scale Types

  • Verbal: "1 cm represents 1 km."
  • Representative Fraction (RF): 1:50,000.
  • Graphical (bar scale): Visual bar. Remains valid when map is resized.
  • Large scale = small area, more detail (1:5,000). Small scale = large area, less detail (1:1,000,000).
Q Past Exam Questions
+Explain types of map projections with examples. (2073, 2076, 2079)

Cylindrical (Mercator), Conical (Lambert), Azimuthal. Explain properties: conformal, equal-area, equidistant. Draw projection surfaces.

+What is MUTM? How is it different from standard UTM? (2074, 2075, 2078, 2081)

MUTM: 3-degree zones for Nepal (vs 6-degree UTM). Scale factor 0.9999 (vs 0.9996). More accurate for Nepal's narrow extent. Two zones: 84–87 E and 87–90 E.

+Differentiate between GCS and PCS. (2072, 2079, 2080)

GCS: angular (degrees), 3D reference. PCS: linear (metres), flat plane. GCS = location reference; PCS = measurement system. Use comparison table.

+What is RMS error? Given control points, calculate RMS. (2074 Bhadra, 2076, 2082)

RMS = sqrt(sum of squared residuals / n). Show calculation step by step. Compare with acceptable threshold.

+Distinguish accuracy and precision with diagram. (2071, 2075)

Draw target diagrams: (a) accurate + precise, (b) precise + inaccurate, (c) accurate + imprecise, (d) neither. Explain systematic vs random error.

+Explain types of map scales with examples. (2073, 2078)

Verbal, RF, graphical. Graphical best because it resizes with map. Large scale = large detail, small area.

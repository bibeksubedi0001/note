1 Three Segments

Space Segment

  • 24+ satellites, 6 orbital planes, ~20,200 km altitude, 12-hour orbit.
  • Broadcasts on L1 (1575.42 MHz) and L2 (1227.60 MHz).

Control Segment

  • 1 Master Control Station (Colorado Springs) + 5 Monitor Stations + 3 Ground Antennas.
  • Tracks satellites, computes orbits, uploads corrections.

User Segment

  • GPS receivers: handheld, survey-grade, vehicle-mounted.

2 Trilateration

  • Distance = Speed of light × Travel time.
  • 1 satellite = sphere. 2 = circle. 3 = two points. 4th = unique point + clock correction.
  • Minimum 4 satellites: 3 for X,Y,Z + 1 for clock bias.

3 DGPS and RTK

  • Base station at known location computes error, sends correction to rover.
  • Real-time DGPS: Corrections via radio/cellular. Accuracy 1–3 m.
  • RTK: Carrier phase. Centimetre-level accuracy (1–2 cm).

5 GDOP

  • Low GDOP (1–2) = satellites spread = accurate. High (>6) = clustered = poor.
  • Types: PDOP (position), HDOP (horizontal), VDOP (vertical), TDOP (time).

6 GPS-GIS Integration

  1. Plan survey (accuracy, coordinate system).
  2. Collect GPS data with attributes.
  3. Differential correction.
  4. Transfer and coordinate transformation.
  5. Import into GIS, overlay, analyse.
Q Past Exam Questions
+Explain the components of GPS (three segments). (2071–2082, 4–8 marks)

Space: 24+ sats, 6 planes, 20,200 km. Control: MCS + monitors + antennas. User: receivers. Draw signal flow diagram.

+How does GPS work? Explain trilateration. (2074, 2075, 2079)

Distance = c × dt. Sphere intersection. Draw diagram with 4 satellites and receiver.

+Explain DGPS and how it improves accuracy. (2073, 2074, 2076, 2080, 2081)

Base station computes error, sends correction. Standalone 5–10 m → DGPS 1–3 m → RTK 1–2 cm.

+Sources of errors in GPS and their corrections. (2072, 2075, 2078, 2079, 2081, 2082)

Use error table. Seven sources with magnitude and correction for each.

+What is GDOP? Why is satellite geometry important? (2079 Chaitra)

GDOP quantifies geometry effect on accuracy. Low = good, High = poor. Types: PDOP, HDOP, VDOP, TDOP.

+Steps of integrating GPS with GIS. (2071, 2072)

Plan, collect, correct, transfer, transform, import, overlay, analyse.

2. Terai Belt Asked ALL 5 exams

2.1 Geological Succession (top → bottom)

  1. Topsoil — agricultural layer
  2. Shallow aquifer — unconfined, 10–30 m, fine–medium sand
  3. Clay aquitard — separating layer
  4. Deep aquifer — confined, coarse sand/gravel, 50–500 m depth

2.2 Three Hydrological Zones

ZoneT (m²/day)Key Feature
Bhabar (foothills)>5000Boulders/cobbles; primary recharge zone; water table deep
Central Terai2000–6000Most productive; alternating sand-gravel-clay; artesian in places
Southern Terai500–2000Finer sediments; lower yield; quality concerns

2.3 Shallow vs. Deep Aquifer Comparison

2.4 Development Status & Possibilities

  • 40,000+ STWs, 10,000+ DTWs installed; only 20–25% of GW developed
  • Roles: Drinking water, agriculture (Nepal's breadbasket), industrial cooling, ecosystem base flow
  • Possibilities: Irrigation expansion, conjunctive use (SW+GW), urban water supply, industrial, MAR (Managed Aquifer Recharge), tourism/spring augmentation

3. Kathmandu Valley Asked 4/5 exams

3.1 Geological Setting

  • Intermontane basin 25 × 20 km; filled by lacustrine/fluvial sediments from Paleo-Kathmandu Lake

3.2 Three-Layer Hydrogeological Model

LayerDescriptionHydrogeology
Layer 1 (Shallow)Sand/silt, 5–20 mUnconfined; seasonal fluctuation; vulnerable to contamination
Kalimati ClayBlack organic lacustrine clay, up to 200 m thickPrincipal aquitard; very low permeability; separates shallow & deep systems
Layer 2 (Deep)Coarse sand/gravel, 60–300 mConfined; main supply aquifer; limited recharge through thick clay
Layer 3 (Basal)Basal gravel (where present)Most productive where it exists

3.3 Spatial Variation

ZoneCharacter
Northern (Shivapuri alluvial fan)Coarse sediments, good recharge, better quality, main recharge zone
Central/SouthernThick Kalimati Clay, confined aquifer, limited recharge, declining heads, quality issues (ammonia, iron)

3.4 Current Issues (write all for 4+ marks)

  1. Over-extraction: Abstraction 2–3× recharge; water level declined 20–30 m since 1980s
  2. Water quality: Ammonia, iron, coliform, arsenic in deep wells
  3. Land subsidence: 2–5 cm/year in central area
  4. Unregulated extraction: Thousands of unregistered private wells
  5. Spring discharge decline: Due to lowered water table

3.5 Sustainable Strategies

  • Managed Aquifer Recharge (MAR) — recharge ponds, injection wells
  • Regulation & licensing of extraction
  • Conjunctive use with Melamchi surface water
  • Monitoring network expansion
  • Rainwater harvesting for recharge

4. Urban GW Issues — Butwal Case Study 2072 Magh 6 marks

Setting

Alluvial aquifers along Tinau River; wells 10–120 m depth; dry-season drawdown 3–8 m.

Problems

  • Coliform contamination from poor sanitation
  • Unregulated pumping → declining water levels
  • Seasonal water shortage (dry season stress)
  • Lack of monitoring data

Mitigation Measures (6-point answer)

  1. Regulation: Licensing & permitting of wells
  2. MAR: Recharge structures along Tinau River
  3. Rainwater harvesting: Supplement recharge
  4. Conjunctive use: Combine surface & groundwater
  5. Wellhead protection zones: Prevent contamination
  6. Monitoring & awareness: Regular water level & quality monitoring; public education; improved sanitation

5. Numerical Formulas Asked 2/5 exams

5.1 Annual GW Storage Change

\[ V = S \times A \times \Delta h \]

where S = storativity, A = area (m²), Δh = water level change (m), V = volume (m³).

5.2 Number of Wells Required

\[ n = \frac{V}{q \times t} \]

where q = pump discharge (m³/hr), t = pumping hours/year.

5.3 Solved Example 1 — Pokhara

Given: S = 0.0008, A = 800 km² = 800×10⁶ m², Δh = 10 m
       q = 30 m³/hr, pumping = 200 days × 24 hr = 4800 hr

V = 0.0008 × 800×10⁶ × 10 = 6,400,000 m³ = 6.4 × 10⁶ m³

n = 6,400,000 / (30 × 4800) = 6,400,000 / 144,000 ≈ 44 wells

5.4 Solved Example 2 — Janakpur

Given: S = 0.0009, A = 850 km² = 850×10⁶ m², Δh = 5 m
       q = 30 m³/hr, pumping = 210 days × 24 hr = 5040 hr

V = 0.0009 × 850×10⁶ × 5 = 3,825,000 m³ = 3.825 × 10⁶ m³

n = 3,825,000 / (30 × 5040) = 3,825,000 / 151,200 ≈ 25 wells

6.1 Definition, Causes & Impacts of Climate Change 208120802079207820762075207420732072207120702069

Definitions

  • Weather: Day-to-day atmospheric conditions (temp, rain, wind)
  • Climate: Average weather over 30+ years
  • Climate Change: Long-term shift in global/regional climate patterns due to natural and anthropogenic factors
  • Climate Variability: Short-term fluctuations around the mean (El Niño, monsoon variation)

Causes (Factors Contributing to Climate Change)

  1. GHG Emissions: CO₂ (fossil fuel), CH₄ (livestock, rice paddies), N₂O (fertilizers), CFCs
  2. Deforestation: Reduced CO₂ absorption; releases stored carbon
  3. Industrialization: Factories, power plants burning fossil fuels
  4. Urbanization: Heat islands, increased energy demand
  5. Agricultural Practices: Methane from livestock; nitrous oxide from fertilizers
  6. Waste Decomposition: Landfill methane emissions

Climate Change Gases 2076

GasSourceGWP (CO₂=1)
CO₂Fossil fuels, deforestation1
CH₄Livestock, rice, landfills28
N₂OFertilizers, industry265
CFCs/HFCsRefrigerants, aerosols1,000–10,000

Impacts

  1. Temperature Rise: +1.1°C since pre-industrial; heatwaves more frequent
  2. Sea Level Rise: ~3.6 mm/yr → coastal flooding, island submergence
  3. Extreme Events: Intense floods, droughts, cyclones, forest fires
  4. Biodiversity: Coral bleaching, species migration, extinction
  5. Agriculture: Crop failure, pest spread, water scarcity
  6. Health: Heat stress, tropical diseases spreading northward
  7. Economy: Damage costs, climate refugees, infrastructure loss

Nepal-Specific Impacts 208020742073

  • GLOF Risk: 21+ potentially dangerous glacial lakes (Tsho Rolpa, Imja)
  • Temperature: Rising at 0.06°C/yr (faster in mountains than plains)
  • Monsoon: Erratic — intense rain events; longer dry spells
  • Snow-line Recession: Himalayas losing ~0.5 m/yr snow line
  • Agriculture: Rice, maize yield declining; new pest zones
  • Water: Dry-season river flow decreasing

“Think Globally, Act Locally” 2081

  • Global problem requires local action — plant trees, save energy, reduce waste
  • Community forestry, biogas, ICS — Nepal’s local contributions to global effort

6.2 Mitigation Measures of Climate Change 2078207320722069

Mitigation (Reducing Causes)

  1. Renewable Energy: Solar, wind, hydro replacing fossil fuels
  2. Energy Efficiency: LED lighting, green buildings, efficient appliances
  3. Afforestation: Planting trees to absorb CO₂
  4. Carbon Pricing: Carbon tax, cap-and-trade systems
  5. Clean Transport: Electric vehicles, mass transit, cycling infrastructure
  6. Industrial Reform: Cleaner production, carbon capture & storage (CCS)
  7. Waste Management: Reduce, reuse, recycle; landfill methane capture

Adaptation (Coping with Impacts) 2073

  1. Early Warning Systems: Flood forecasting, GLOF monitoring
  2. Climate-Resilient Infrastructure: Raised roads, flood-resistant buildings
  3. Drought-Resistant Crops: Agricultural adaptation
  4. Water Harvesting: Rainwater collection for dry seasons
  5. Insurance: Crop insurance, disaster insurance

Nepal’s Perspective

  • Contributes < 0.1% of global GHGs but highly vulnerable
  • Community forestry (22,000+ groups managing 2.2M ha)
  • Biogas & ICS programs reducing biomass burning
  • LAPA (Local Adaptation Plans of Action) in 100+ municipalities

6.4 Bio-Gas & Organic Farming 20792077

Biogas

  • Process: Anaerobic digestion of cattle dung, human waste, crop residue → CH₄ + CO₂ (60:40)
  • Output: Clean gas for cooking + slurry as organic fertilizer
  • Nepal: 400,000+ household plants (BSP program); mainly Terai and Mid-hills
  • Benefits: Replaces firewood (saves forests), reduces indoor smoke (reduces ARI), produces fertilizer, reduces GHGs

Suitability in Terai 2077

  • Warm climate (optimal at 30–40°C for digestion)
  • Large cattle population (dung readily available)
  • Flat terrain (easy construction)
  • Slurry directly used in adjacent farmland

Organic Farming

  • No synthetic fertilizers/pesticides; use compost, green manure, biogas slurry
  • Reduces soil degradation & chemical runoff
  • Sequesters carbon in soil (improves soil organic matter)
  • Nepal: growing organic tea, coffee, spice exports (premium pricing)

How They Help Minimize Climate Change 2079

  1. Replace fossil fuels with renewable biogas → reduce CO₂
  2. Replace firewood → reduce deforestation → maintain CO₂ sinks
  3. Organic farming reduces N₂O from synthetic fertilizers
  4. Soil carbon sequestration through composting
  5. Methane capture in biogas plants (instead of uncontrolled release from open dung piles)

6.5 Deforestation and Its Consequences 2077

Causes in Nepal

  • Firewood collection (80% rural energy)
  • Agricultural expansion (slash-and-burn)
  • Infrastructure development (roads, settlements)
  • Illegal logging and encroachment
  • Forest fires (dry season)

Consequences

  1. Climate: Reduced CO₂ absorption; releases stored carbon
  2. Soil Erosion: Loss of root binding → landslides (critical in hills)
  3. Flooding: Reduced water retention capacity → flash floods
  4. Biodiversity: Habitat destruction → species extinction
  5. Water Cycle: Reduced transpiration → less local rainfall
  6. Livelihood: Loss of forest products (timber, NTFPs, medicines)

6.1 Definition, Causes & Impacts 20812080207820762075207420732072207120702069

Weather vs Climate

WeatherClimate
State of atmosphere at any instantLong-term pattern of weather in a particular area
Short term, changes rapidlySeasonal change over decades
Difficult to predict long-termPredictable based on historical data

Key Definitions

  • Climate Change: Long-term alteration of temperature and weather patterns caused by natural factors (volcanic eruptions, ocean currents, orbital changes) + artificial factors (GHG emissions from human activities)
  • Climate Variability: Natural short-term fluctuations (year-to-year, decade-to-decade) like El Niño/La Niña — NOT the same as climate change

Extreme Events

  1. Droughts: Higher evapotranspiration → desertification
  2. Floods: Extreme precipitation in low-lying areas
  3. Hurricanes/Cyclones: Increased intensity from warmer oceans
  4. GLOFs: Glacial retreat in Himalayas threatens catastrophic flooding
  5. Coastal Flooding: Sea level rose ~8 inches last century (3.2 mm/yr)

Causes — Natural

  1. Volcanic Eruptions: Release GHGs, ashes, dust
  2. Wind & Ocean Currents: Conveyor belts transporting heat
  3. Orbital Changes: Tilt 21.5°–24.5° (41,000 yr); orbit shape (100,000 yr)
  4. Forest Fires: Destroy CO₂ consumers
  5. Meteor Impacts: Debris blocks sunlight

Causes — Artificial (Human)

  1. Deforestation (reduced CO₂ absorption)
  2. Burning fossil fuels (factories, power plants)
  3. Excessive automobiles
  4. Suburbanization / urban sprawl

8 Impacts of Climate Change

  1. Temperature Rise: +1.1°C since 19th century; may warm 4°C within 83 years
  2. Sea Level Rise: ~8 inches last century (3.2 mm/yr)
  3. Glacier Melting: Greenland lost 150–250 km³/yr (2002–2006); Antarctica ~152 km³ (2002–2005)
  4. Arctic Ice Loss: Extent and thickness decreased significantly
  5. Extreme Events: Droughts, intense rainfall, floods, hurricanes
  6. Ocean Acidification: Surface ocean acidity +30% since industrial revolution; 2 billion tons CO₂/yr absorbed
  7. Species Migration & Extinction: Tropical diseases migrate with warming
  8. Health Risks: Vector-borne diseases, heat-related illness

Impacts in Nepal 208020742073

  1. Glacial Retreat & GLOFs: Himalayan glaciers retreating; 1.054 billion tons carbon in forests
  2. Erratic Rainfall: Changed monsoon patterns → floods, landslides, drought
  3. Agriculture Impact: Changed growing seasons, crop production decline
  4. Biodiversity Loss: Species migration, habitat disruption
  5. Water Scarcity: Reduced snowfall & glacier melt threatening rivers
  6. Health Risks: Malaria, dengue spreading to higher elevations
  7. Deforestation: Forest cover: 57% (1947) → 40.36% (2015)
  8. NAPA: Launched 2010; historic Everest cabinet meeting (Dec 4, 2009)

“Think Globally, Act Locally” 2081

  • Local afforestation & community forestry
  • Clean energy: biogas, solar, improved cooking stoves
  • Waste segregation, composting, reduce open burning
  • Cycling, public transport, EVs
  • Sustainable/organic farming → reduce N₂O & CH₄
  • Watershed management & rainwater harvesting
  • Nepal’s global emissions ~0.027%, but local actions build national resilience

6.3 International Efforts 208020792078207720762075207320702069

1. UNFCCC (1992)

  • Goal: Stabilize GHG concentrations to prevent dangerous anthropogenic interference
  • Adopted 1992, in force March 21, 1994; 196 countries
  • Works through annual COP (Conference of Parties)
  • Provides funding, insurance, technology transfer

2. Kyoto Protocol (1997)

  • Negotiated Dec 1997, Kyoto, Japan; in force Feb 16, 2005
  • Committed developed countries to reduce GHG by 5.2% by 2012 (vs 1990)
  • Targets 6 GHGs: CO₂, CH₄, N₂O, PFCs, HFCs, SF₆

Three Mechanisms:

  1. ETS — Emissions Trading System (Assigned Amount Units)
  2. CDM — Clean Development Mechanism (invest in mitigation projects in developing countries)
  3. JI — Joint Implementation (emission reduction between developed & transition countries)

3. Cancun Adaptation Framework (COP 16, 2010)

  • Adaptation given same priority as mitigation
  • Country-driven, gender-sensitive, participatory
  • Guided by best science & indigenous knowledge
  • Implement NAPAs; build resilience in vulnerable countries

4. Green Climate Fund

  • Established COP 16 (2010), operational COP 17 (Durban, 2011)
  • Supports developing countries via CDM, adaptation programs
  • Includes Adaptation Fund, GEF Trust Fund, LDCF, SCCF

Nepal’s Alignment 20802079

  1. NAPA implementation (launched 2010)
  2. CDM investments in hydropower, biogas, forestry
  3. REDD+ leverage: 40.36% forest, 1.054B tons carbon
  4. GCF access for adaptation infrastructure
  5. Scale up biogas (since 1974/75), solar, micro-hydro
  6. 10 national parks + 6 conservation areas as carbon sinks

6.4 Bio-Gas & Organic Farming 20792077

Biogas

  • Composition: CH₄ 50–70%, CO₂ 30–40%, H₂ 5–10%
  • Properties: Sp. gravity 0.55; ignition 650°C; 6 kWh/m³; digestion 20–35°C; retention 40–100 days
  • Uses: Electricity/heat, vehicle fuel (pure), organic fertilizer

How Biogas Minimizes Climate Change

  1. Reduces deforestation (replaces fuelwood)
  2. Burns cleaner; captures CH₄ (25× more potent than CO₂)
  3. Decentralized energy reduces fossil fuel dependence
  4. Reduces indoor air pollution
  5. Self-sustaining through fuel & fertilizer savings

Biogas in Terai 2077

  • Warm tropical climate ideal (20–35°C year-round)
  • High livestock concentration → abundant manure
  • Agricultural heartland → fertilizer by-product useful
  • Flat terrain → easy construction
  • Reduces Chure range deforestation pressure

Organic Farming 2079

  • Uses natural nutrient sources (manure, compost, crop residue) instead of synthetic agrochemicals
  • Nitrogen via legumes & biological fixation; pest control by rotations & natural predators
  • Climate benefits: Reduces N₂O (GH efficiency 230× CO₂); improves soil carbon sequestration; no chemical pollution; nutritious food

6.5 Deforestation & Consequences 2077

Key Facts

  • Present forest: 30% of world’s land (~3.9B hectares)
  • Loss: 18.7 million acres/year (~27 football fields/min)
  • 2nd largest anthropogenic CO₂ source (after fossil fuels)
  • 1.5 billion tons CO₂ released annually; 15% of all GHG emissions (WWF)
  • At current rate: no rainforest in ~100 years

8 Causes

  1. Agriculture (major cause)
  2. Logging & fuelwood (2.6B people use biomass)
  3. Overgrazing (20% livestock rise 1985–1998)
  4. Fire (19.8M ha/yr affected)
  5. Mining
  6. Urbanization & infrastructure
  7. Illegal timber (Nepal-India, Nepal-China borders)
  8. Tourism, political misuse, natural calamities

Consequences

  • Climate Change: ~300B tons carbon in trees (40× annual fossil fuel GHG)
  • Biodiversity Loss: 80% of species in tropical rainforests
  • Water Depletion, Soil Erosion, Landslides
  • Social: Lost ecological services; tribal displacement
  • Ocean Acidification from higher atmospheric CO₂

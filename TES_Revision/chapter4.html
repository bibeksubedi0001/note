4.1 Definition of Environment 20732070

  • Environment: The totality of all external conditions (physical, chemical, biological, social) affecting an organism.
  • Ecosystem: A community of living organisms (biotic) interacting with their non-living components (abiotic) in a functional unit. Energy flows & nutrient cycles bind them.
  • Ecology: The scientific study of relationships between organisms and their environment.

Components of Environment

ComponentExamples
LithosphereLand, soil, minerals, rocks
HydrosphereWater (rivers, lakes, oceans, groundwater)
AtmosphereAir, gases, weather, climate
BiosphereAll living organisms

4.2 Importance, Ecology & Ecosystem 20742072207120702069

Ecosystem Characteristics

  • Producers: Plants, algae (convert solar energy)
  • Consumers: Herbivores, carnivores, omnivores
  • Decomposers: Bacteria, fungi (recycle nutrients)
  • Energy Flow: Sun → Producer → Primary Consumer → Secondary Consumer → Decomposer

Human Intervention in Ecosystem 20742072

  1. Deforestation: Habitat loss, soil erosion, CO₂ increase
  2. Urbanization: Concrete replacing green spaces, heat islands
  3. Agriculture: Monoculture, pesticides reducing biodiversity
  4. Industry: Pollution of air, water, soil
  5. Population Growth: Increased resource demand beyond carrying capacity

Natural Resources Converted to Wealth

  • Forests → timber, furniture, paper
  • Water → hydropower, irrigation, fisheries
  • Minerals → construction, industry, exports
  • Tourism (natural beauty → economic revenue)

4.4 Optimum Utilization of Natural Resources 20812079

Optimum utilization: Using natural resources efficiently to meet present needs without compromising future generations’ ability to meet theirs.

  • Balance between development and conservation
  • Efficient irrigation (drip, sprinkler) vs flood irrigation
  • Sustainable forestry (selective logging, replanting)
  • Renewable energy over fossil fuels
  • Integrated water resource management (IWRM)

4.6 Conflict of Resources 20812078

Conflict of Resources: Disagreement among stakeholders over the use, ownership or distribution of limited natural resources.

Examples

  • Hydropower vs Agriculture: Dam reservoirs submerge fertile farmland
  • Industry vs Community: Factory effluent polluting drinking water sources
  • Urbanization vs Forest: Expanding cities encroaching on forest land
  • Tourism vs Conservation: Trekking/mountaineering degrading fragile alpine ecosystems
  • Upstream vs Downstream: Water diversion depriving downstream users (Melamchi project)

4.7 Global Environmental Issues 20792077

  1. Global Warming: Rising CO₂ & GHGs → temperature increase → ice melt, sea rise
  2. Ozone Depletion: CFCs destroying O₃ layer → UV radiation increase
  3. Biodiversity Loss: Species extinction at 100–1000x natural rate
  4. Acid Rain: SO₂ & NOx emissions → acidic precipitation → forest/lake damage
  5. Deforestation: 10M hectares lost/yr globally; CO₂ sink reduction
  6. Ocean Pollution: Plastic, oil spills, eutrophication
  7. Desertification: Land degradation in arid/semi-arid regions

Driving Forces of Global Warming 2077

  • Fossil fuel combustion (CO₂)
  • Industrial processes (CH₄, N₂O)
  • Deforestation (reduced CO₂ absorption)
  • Agriculture (methane from rice paddies, livestock)

4.8 Environmental Issues of Nepal 20802074207220702069

Major Issues

  1. Air Pollution: Kathmandu Valley PM2.5 exceeds WHO limits; brick kilns, vehicles
  2. Water Pollution: Bagmati, Bishnumati rivers severely polluted; untreated sewage
  3. Deforestation: Chure hills degradation, landslides, Terai flooding
  4. Solid Waste: Sisdol landfill — inadequate; open dumping, no segregation
  5. Soil Erosion & Landslides: Steep terrain + deforestation + monsoon
  6. Climate Change: Glacial retreat, erratic rainfall, GLOF risks
  7. Loss of Biodiversity: Poaching (tiger, rhino), habitat encroachment

Causes

  • Rapid urbanization without planning
  • Industrial effluent without treatment
  • Population pressure on limited arable land
  • Weak enforcement of environmental laws
  • Poverty → unsustainable resource use

Kathmandu Valley Specific 2080

  • Brick kilns (Bull’s Trench Kilns) → major PM source
  • Vehicle emissions (old diesel vehicles)
  • Road dust (unpaved roads)
  • Open burning of waste
  • Encroachment of rivers & wetlands

Acid Rain 2072

  • SO₂ + NOx from vehicles/industries react with water vapor
  • Form H₂SO₄ / HNO₃ → acidic precipitation (pH < 5.6)
  • Damages forests, aquatic life, buildings, monuments
  • Nepal: mainly from brick kilns & vehicle emissions in Kathmandu

4.1 Definition of Environment 20732070

Environment: Surroundings of all living and non-living things. ISO: “surroundings in which an organization operates including air, water, land, natural resources, flora, fauna, humans and their inter-relations.”

Ecosystem: A grouping of organisms that interact with each other and their environment, including both biotic (living) and abiotic (non-living) components functioning as a unit.

5 Components of Environment

  1. Hydrosphere — Water
  2. Atmosphere — Air
  3. Lithosphere — Land
  4. Biosphere — Flora, Fauna, Microbes
  5. Anthrosphere — Man-made things
  • Biotic: Producers (autotrophs—plants), Consumers (heterotrophs—animals), Decomposers (fungi, bacteria)
  • Abiotic: Climatic (temperature, light, moisture) and Edaphic (soil-related) factors

4.2 Ecology & Ecosystem 20742072207120702069

6 Characteristics of Ecosystem

  1. Structure: Biotic + abiotic components
  2. Energy Flow: Unidirectional — sun → producers → consumers
  3. Nutrient Cycling: C, N, P, S cycle between organisms and environment
  4. Dynamic Nature: Constant change through succession
  5. Self-Regulating: Homeostasis via feedback (predator-prey)
  6. Interdependence: All components interconnected (holocoenotic)

Population Disturbance to Ecosystem

  • Excessive fossil fuels → GHG & global warming
  • Deforestation → destroying carbon sinks
  • Overfishing, overgrazing
  • Industrial waste in water, air, soil
  • Biodiversity loss & species extinction
  • Excessive agrochemicals

8 Reasons for Human Intervention 20742072

  1. Population Growth
  2. Economic Development
  3. Energy Needs
  4. Agricultural Expansion
  5. Resource Extraction
  6. Urbanization
  7. Technological Advancement
  8. Consumption Patterns

Conversion of Resources to Wealth 2072

  • Agriculture: Soil, water, sunlight → crops & livestock
  • Energy: Fossil fuels & renewables → electricity
  • Mining: Minerals, metals, building materials
  • Forestry: Timber, medicinal plants
  • Water: Irrigation, hydropower, fisheries
  • Tourism: Natural landscapes & biodiversity

4.3 Conservation of Environment

Conservation: Wise management and utilization of natural resources in sustainable manner to ensure biodiversity maintenance.

4 Types of Conservation

  1. Energy: Reduce consumption, use renewables
  2. Water: Reduce usage, recycle wastewater
  3. Soil: Prevent erosion, chemical contamination
  4. Plant: Conserve rare/endangered plants, control invasive species

4.4 Optimum Utilization of Natural Resources 20812079

Allocate natural resources to best use — satisfying human needs at minimum cost on sustainable basis.

Types of Resources

  • Perpetual: Virtually inexhaustible (solar, wind)
  • Renewable: Available over time; can be produced (forests, fisheries)
  • Non-renewable: Fixed amounts; exhausted after time (some recyclable)

Nepal Examples

  • Community Forestry: 29% (1994) → 40.36% (2015)
  • Micro-Hydro: 3,000+ plants serving rural Nepal
  • Solar energy replacing diesel generators
  • Sustainable agriculture: crop rotation, organic farming, IPM
  • Rainwater harvesting for dry-season use

4.6 Conflict of Resources 20812078

Definition: Situation where allocation, management, or use of natural resources results in violence, human rights abuses, or denial of access. ~40% of international conflicts due to resource disputes.

Causes

  1. Rapid population growth → environmental degradation
  2. Social inequality → skewed resource access
  3. Failure of governing bodies
  4. Unsustainable consumption
  5. Economic globalization

Conflicts in Nepal

  • Land: Boundary disputes, ownership changes, tenant eviction
  • Forest: Ownership disputes, product access, illegal collection, poaching
  • Water: Source disputes, drinking/irrigation sharing, compensation for water projects

4.8 Environmental Issues of Nepal 20802074207220702069

6 Major Issues

  1. Climate Change & Warming: Glacial melt, rainfall pattern changes
  2. Air & Water Pollution: Untreated sewage, arsenic (Terai), vehicular emissions
  3. Deforestation & Land Degradation: Overgrazing, floods, soil erosion
  4. Illegal Mining: Sand/stone → soil instability, water table lowering
  5. Poaching: Illegal wildlife hunting → endangered species extinction
  6. Water-borne Diseases: Cholera, diarrhea, typhoid; 7,882 diarrheal deaths (WHO 2017)

Kathmandu Valley 2080

  • Air pollution (vehicular, industrial, brick kilns)
  • Water pollution (untreated sewage in Bagmati)
  • Haphazard urbanization
  • Solid waste management crisis

Causes & Mitigation

IssueMitigation
Climate ChangeRenewable energy, forestation, emission reduction
Air/Water PollutionEmission standards, wastewater treatment, ban old vehicles
DeforestationAfforestation, community forestry, sustainable agriculture
Illegal MiningRegulation, law enforcement, alternative materials
PoachingNational park protection, anti-poaching laws
Waterborne DiseaseWater treatment, sanitation, health education

Acid Rain 2072

  • SO₂ + NO₂ → sulfuric & nitric acids mixed with rain
  • Sources: Fossil fuel burning, vehicular emissions, industrial processes, volcanoes
  • Effects: Forest damage, lake acidification, infrastructure corrosion, soil nutrient leaching

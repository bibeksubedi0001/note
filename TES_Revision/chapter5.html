5.5 Organic Pollution 20812073

Organic Pollutants: Carbon-based substances (sewage, manure, food waste, plant debris) that consume dissolved oxygen when decomposed in water.

Key Indicators

  • BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand): O₂ consumed by microbes in 5 days at 20°C
  • COD (Chemical Oxygen Demand): Total O₂ needed for chemical oxidation
  • DO (Dissolved Oxygen): Healthy water ≥ 6 mg/L; polluted < 4 mg/L

Aerobic vs Anaerobic Digestion 2073

AerobicAnaerobic
Requires O₂No O₂ needed
Faster decompositionSlower but produces biogas (CH₄)
Produces CO₂ + H₂OProduces CH₄ + CO₂
Used in: activated sludge, trickling filtersUsed in: biogas plants, septic tanks
Less odorFoul odor if not sealed

5.9 Indoor Air Pollution 20812071

Causes

  • Biomass burning (firewood, dung, crop residue) in poorly ventilated kitchens
  • Kerosene lamps; mosquito coils; tobacco smoke
  • Building materials (asbestos, paints, adhesives releasing VOCs)

Impacts

  • ARI (Acute Respiratory Infections) — #1 cause of child mortality in Nepal

  • Women & children most affected (spend most time in kitchen)

Mitigation in Nepal Context 2081

  2. Improved Cook Stoves (ICS): Reduce smoke by 50–70%
  3. Ventilation: Smoke hoods, chimneys, windows
  4. Solar Home Systems: Replace kerosene for lighting
  5. LPG Promotion: Subsidized LPG distribution in rural areas

5.1 Fecal-Oral Infection Transmission (F-Diagram) 208020762072

Pathway where pathogens from infected person’s feces reach new host’s mouth → cholera, typhoid, dysentery, diarrhea, hepatitis A, polio.

5 F’s Pathways

  1. Fluids (Water): Contaminated water for drinking/cooking/washing
  2. Fields (Soil): Open defecation contaminates soil & crops
  3. Flies: Carry pathogens from feces to exposed food
  4. Fingers: Unwashed hands after defecation
  5. Food: Prepared with unwashed hands or polluted water

Barriers

  • Primary (prevent leaving feces): Sanitary latrines, proper sewage disposal, safe excreta disposal
  • Secondary (prevent reaching host): Water treatment (boiling, chlorination, filtration), hand washing with soap, food hygiene, fly control, health education

Key Pathogens

  • Bacteria: Salmonella, Shigella, V. cholerae, Campylobacter
  • Viruses: Hepatitis A/E, Polio, Rotavirus
  • Parasites: Entamoeba, Giardia, Cryptosporidium, tapeworms

5.2 Preventive Measures 2076

  • Safe Water: Piped treated supply; household treatment (boiling, filtration, chlorination, SODIS)
  • Sanitation: Sanitary latrines (pour-flush, VIP, eco-san); CLTS programs, ODF zones
  • Hygiene: Hand washing with soap; proper food handling; clean utensils
  • Environmental: Solid waste management; drainage; vector control
  • Health Education: Community awareness; school programs; mass media

5.3 On-Site Sanitation & Eco-Sanitation

On-Site Systems

  • Pit Latrines: Traditional pit; VIP with fly screens & vent pipes
  • Pour-Flush: Water-seal U-trap → soak pit or twin-pit
  • Septic Tanks: Underground anaerobic digestion → soak pit
  • Composting Toilets: Dry; feces decomposed with ash/sawdust

Eco-Sanitation (ECOSAN)

Closed-loop sanitation: excreta recycled, rendered safe, used for agriculture. Prevents pollution rather than controlling after occurrence.

  • UDDT: Urine-Diverting Dry Toilet — separates urine/feces; feces composted, urine diluted for fertilizer
  • Twin-Pit Alternating: Two pits used alternately
  • Biogas-Linked: Excreta → biogas digester → methane + slurry

Advantages: Minimal water; nutrients (N, P, K) recycled; no groundwater contamination; suitable for water-scarce/flood-prone areas.

5.4 Importance of Health Education 20732072

  • Behavioral change: adoption of hand washing, safe water storage, toilet use
  • Disease prevention: reduces cholera, typhoid, diarrhea incidence
  • Community participation in sanitation programs (CLTS, ODF)
  • Sustainability through knowledge-based maintenance
  • Reduces child mortality (ORS, breastfeeding, nutrition)
  • Women empowerment (menstrual hygiene, maternal health)

Nepal Status: Poor — low literacy, poor health services, superstitions, lack of social responsibility.

5.7 Sources, Causes & Impacts of Air Pollution 207920782077207520732072207120702069

Definition: Presence of substances in atmosphere injurious to human health, property, and life.

10 Sources

  1. Fossil fuel burning (CO₂, CO, NOₓ, SO₂)
  2. Motor vehicles (NOₓ increased 690% from 1900–1970)
  3. Incomplete fuel burning (VOCs)
  4. Garbage/landfill (methane)
  5. Natural sources (forest fires)
  6. Dust particles (soil erosion)
  7. Radioactive fallout
  8. Volcanoes (SO₂, volcanic ash)
  9. Smog (temperature inversion)
  10. Powerlines (ionization → free radicals)

7 Pollutant Types

  • PM₁₀, PM₂.₅: Diesel, brick kilns, construction dust, biomass
  • CO: Incomplete combustion in vehicles
  • SO₂: Coal/petroleum burning
  • NOₓ: High-temperature combustion
  • VOCs: Fuel evaporation, solvents
  • Lead (Pb): Leaded gasoline (now largely banned)
  • Ozone (O₃): Secondary pollutant from NOₓ + VOCs + sunlight

Impacts

  • Health: Asthma, bronchitis, COPD, lung cancer, cardiovascular disease, eye irritation; WHO: 2.4M deaths/yr; Nepal ARI >30% child deaths
  • Environmental: Acid rain, ozone depletion, global warming (1.4–5.8°C rise projected), smog
  • Economic: Healthcare costs, agricultural loss, monument damage, reduced tourism

Acid Rain 2069

  • Normal rain pH ~5.6; acid rain <5.0 (sometimes 3.0)
  • Sources: Power plants (70% SO₂), motor vehicles (43% NOₓ)
  • Impacts: Aquatic life death at pH <5.0; forest damage; crop damage; building corrosion; groundwater contamination
  • Control: Low-sulfur fuels; scrubbers (flue gas desulphurization); catalytic converters; liming acidified lakes; cap-and-trade programs

5.8 Mitigation Measures 2079207820752074207220712070

Vehicular Emission Control

  • Catalytic converters (CO, HC, NOₓ → CO₂, H₂O, N₂)
  • Electronic fuel injection; charcoal canisters
  • Mandatory emission testing; Green Sticker system
  • Phase out two-stroke engines and old diesel vehicles
  • Electric vehicles, CNG, public transport promotion

Industrial Emission Control

  • Scrubbers (flue gas desulphurization)
  • Bag filters, electrostatic precipitators
  • Cleaner fuels (natural gas vs coal)
  • Industrial Enterprises Act (1992) enforcement

Kathmandu-Specific 20782077

  1. Electric bus fleet expansion; cycle lanes
  2. Brick kiln zig-zag tech conversion; relocation
  3. Road dust: regular sweeping, water sprinkling, paving
  4. Ban open burning; proper waste collection/composting
  5. Tree planting; urban forest protection
  6. Air Quality Index (AQI) monitoring system

Water Pollution Mitigation

  1. Mandatory ETP; CETP for industrial clusters
  2. Functional treatment plants; storm-water/sewer separation
  3. Prevent river dumping; proper waste management
  4. Polluter Pays Principle + monitoring penalties
  5. Safe sanitation: VIP, pour-flush, septic; Nepal achieved ODF 2019
  6. IPM, organic farming; vegetation buffer zones
  7. Watershed management, rainwater harvesting

5.9 Indoor Air Pollution 2071

People spend 90% indoors. Indoor levels 2–5× (occasionally 100+×) higher than outdoor.

Nepal Causes

  • Biomass fuel (~90% of total energy): firewood, crop residues, dung
  • Poorly ventilated kitchens (no chimneys/windows)
  • Coal burning (mountain regions)
  • Kerosene lamps, tobacco smoke, incense

Health Effects

  • ARI: leading child death cause <5 in Nepal (>30%)
  • COPD, chronic bronchitis (especially women who cook)
  • Lung cancer: 2–4× higher risk in biomass-burning households
  • Eye irritation, cataracts; low birth weight

Control Measures (Rural Nepal)

  1. ICS: Chimneys vent smoke; reduce fuel 30–50%, smoke 70–80%
  2. Alternative fuels: Biogas (~400,000 plants), LPG, solar cookers
  3. Kitchen ventilation: Windows, smoke hoods; separate kitchen
  4. Behavioral: Dry firewood first; keep children away from cooking area
  5. Electrification: Micro-hydro, solar PV for lighting & cooking
  6. Health education: Awareness targeting women & FCHVs

5.10 Severity and Problems in Nepal 2079

Key Statistics

  • Yale Environment Index 2014: Nepal ranked 177/180 for air quality
  • Kathmandu: Bowl-shaped topography traps pollutants (temperature inversion)
  • 100+ brick kilns in valley; high dry-season pollution
  • Ambient air pollution: 1,600 premature deaths (2005 estimate)
  • NHRC/WHO: 1,926 premature deaths/year (2009)
  • World Bank 2007: Annual health cost USD 21 million (0.29% GDP)

Key Legislation

  • Environmental Protection Act (1996) & Rules (1997)
  • Water Resources Act (1992)
  • Industrial Enterprises Act (1992)
  • Vehicles & Transport Management Act (1993)
  • Nepal Vehicle Mass Emission Standard (2000)
  • Solid Waste Management Act (2011)

Programs

  • Two-stroke engine restriction; leaded gasoline ban
  • Electric vehicles (Safa Tempo in Kathmandu)
  • Bagmati Civilization Development Committee
  • CLTS programs; ODF status 2019
  • National Biogas Program (400,000+ plants)
  • Montreal Protocol ratification (1996)

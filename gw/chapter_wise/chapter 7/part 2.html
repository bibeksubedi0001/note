Well Screens: Purpose & Function

The Intake Device

The well screen is the filtering device acting as the intake. It must allow water to enter freely while holding back the aquifer's sand and gravel.

Technical Functions

  • Structural Support: Must withstand the radial collapse pressure of the formation and overburden.
  • Sand Control: Acts as a physical sieve. Properly designed screens allow < 5 PPM sand content during operation.
  • Hydraulic Efficiency: High open area minimizes entrance head loss ($h_L$) and drawdown.

Screen Types & Hydraulics

Continuous Slot

(Johnson Screen)

Constructed by winding a triangular wire around longitudinal rods. The "V" shape creates a jetting effect for backwashing.

Open Area: 30% - 50%

Hydraulics: Excellent ($\downarrow$ Friction)

Cost: High

Bridge Slot

(Pressed Slot)

Slots are punched in a bridge shape on a flat sheet, then rolled into a tube. Common in spiral welded pipes.

Open Area: 10% - 18%

Strength: High (vertical)

Cost: Moderate

Slotted Pipe

(Locally Fabricated)

Simple cuts made in a standard blind pipe using a saw or oxy-acetylene torch. Rough edges cause high friction.

Open Area: 4% - 8%

Hydraulics: Poor (High $h_L$)

Cost: Lowest

Note: Higher Open Area = Lower Entrance Velocity = Less Drawdown = Lower Pumping Cost.

Entrance Velocity & Hydraulics

The Physics of Flow

As water approaches the well screen slots, flow converges, and velocity increases rapidly.

  • Laminar Flow: Low velocity ($h_L \propto v$). Preferred.
  • Turbulent Flow: High velocity ($h_L \propto v^2$). Inefficient.

The 3 cm/s Limit

Standard design practice (Walton, 1962) limits Entrance Velocity ($V_e$) to:

$\le 3$ cm/sec (0.03 m/s)

Why this limit?
  • Prevents Sand Pumping.
  • Reduces Chemical Precipitation (Incrustation).
  • Prevents Corrosion erosion.

Slot Size Selection Logic

Slot sizes are derived from the Sieve Analysis of the aquifer sample. The goal is to maximize water flow while keeping the "fines" out.

1

Natural Development

Used for coarse sand/gravel aquifers ($C_u > 3$).

Strategy: Allow the finer 40-50% of the formation to be pumped out during the "Development" phase. The remaining coarser particles form a natural highly permeable zone.

Slot Size = $D_{40}$ to $D_{50}$
of the Aquifer Material

If water is corrosive, use $D_{40}$ (conservative).

2

Artificial Gravel Pack

Used for fine, uniform sand ($C_u < 3$).

Strategy: The screen functions to retain the gravel pack. The gravel pack functions to retain the aquifer. The screen does not touch the aquifer directly.

Slot Size = $D_{90}$
of the Gravel Pack Material

Retains 90% of the Pack material.

Gravel Pack: Introduction

Definition

An artificial gravel pack is an annular layer of artificially graded, clean, round, siliceous gravel placed between the well screen and the borehole wall.

Why use it? (Functions)

  • Stabilization: Prevents collapse of fine aquifer material. Without it, fine sand ($C_u<3$) behaves like a liquid.
  • Filtration: Acts as a primary filter. It slows down water before it hits the screen.
  • Permeability: Increases the effective well radius ($R_w$), which reduces drawdown according to Thiem's equation.
Material Specs:
  • Roundness > 0.6 (Smooth river gravel).
  • Quartz/Silica content > 95% (Durable).
  • Thickness: 3" to 8" (Too thin = voids; Too thick = can't clean).

Pack Selection Criteria

Artificial gravel packing increases drilling diameter and cost. It is only used when the aquifer cannot stabilize itself naturally.

Calculate $C_u = \frac{D_{60}}{D_{10}}$
If $C_u \le 3.0$

Pack REQUIRED

Aquifer is Uniform (all grains similar size).

Why? If you pump a uniform aquifer, you cannot create a graded filter because all particles are the same size. They will all pass through the screen or all clog it. We must introduce an external graded material.

Also required if $D_{10} < 0.25$ mm (Very Fine)
If $C_u > 3.0$

Pack NOT Required

Aquifer is Well Graded (mixed sizes).

Why? The aquifer contains small and large particles. By pumping out the small ones ("Development"), the large ones remain around the screen, forming a "Natural Gravel Pack".

Selection Criteria

Pack Design: The P-A Ratio

The size of the gravel pack is designed relative to the aquifer to ensure it holds the sand back (Bridge Theory). We use the Pack-Aquifer Ratio (PAR).

Design Steps

1

Determine Aquifer $D_{50}$

Obtained from Sieve Analysis curve.

2

Calculate Pack $D_{50}$

$$ \text{Pack } D_{50} = (4 \text{ to } 5) \times \text{Aquifer } D_{50} $$

Note: Ratios > 5 allow sand invasion. Ratios < 4 reduce yield.

3

Determine Screen Slot Size

Slot Size = $D_{90}$ of the Pack material (to hold 90% of the pack).

Thickness: 3" to 8" (7.5 - 20 cm)
Material: Siliceous, rounded, smooth

Gravel Pack Placement

Methods of Installation

1. Surface Pouring (Shoveling)

Simply dumping gravel from the top of the casing.

Risk: "Bridging" occurs. Gravel clumps, creating large voids below. Segregation happens (large stones fall faster).

2. Tremie Pipe Method (Standard)

Gravel is fed through small diameter pipes (2-3") reaching the bottom.

  • Lower Tremie pipe to bottom.
  • Feed gravel and water slurry.
  • Raise pipe gradually as pack rises.

Benefit: Uniform pack, no voids, no segregation.

Infiltration Galleries

Horizontal Abstraction

Infiltration galleries are horizontal tunnels or perforated conduits constructed within a permeable aquifer (often riverbeds) to intercept groundwater flow. They rely on "Riverbank Filtration" (RBF).

Design Specifics

  • Depth: Shallow (3-6m) below river bed.
  • Yield ($Q$): $Q = \frac{K L (H^2 - h^2)}{2R}$ (approx). Depends on Length ($L$).
  • Components: Perforated pipe/concrete gallery, graded gravel surround (reverse filter), sump well, pump house.
  • Scour Protection: Must be buried below the maximum scour depth of the river.

Radial Collector Wells

"Ranney Wells"

A large diameter (3-5m) central caisson from which horizontal strainer pipes (radials) are hydraulically jacked out into the aquifer like spokes of a wheel.

  • High Yield: 500 - 2000 L/s. Equivalent to 10+ standard tube wells. Efficient usage of thin aquifers.
  • Construction: The central caisson is sunk first. Radials (30-50m long) are pushed out. Fines are removed through the radials to create a natural pack around them.
  • Use: Mega city water supplies (e.g., Delhi, London) near river banks.

Summary & Key Takeaways

Screen Design Logic

  • Objective: Maximize open area, minimize velocity ($< 3$ cm/s) to prevent sand pumping and incrustation.
  • Slot Size:
    • Natural Dev: $D_{40}$ to $D_{50}$ of Aquifer.
    • Gravel Pack: $D_{90}$ of Pack Material.

Gravel Pack Logic

  • Required for fine, uniform aquifers ($C_u < 3$).
  • Design: Pack Size = 4-5x Aquifer Size. Pack Thickness = 3-8 inches.
  • Placement: Use Tremie pipe to avoid segregation.

"Design is a balance between hydraulic efficiency (high yield) and structural stability (sand control). A well is an engineered structure, not just a hole in the ground."

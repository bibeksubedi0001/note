Open Well vs Tube Well

(2073 Bhadra Q7a, 3 Marks)

Feature Open Well (Dug Well) Tube Well
1. Diameter Large (2m to 10m) Small (0.1m to 0.6m)
2. Depth Shallow (< 20m) Deep (> 30m to 300m+)
3. Yield (Discharge) Low (3 - 5 lit/s) High (40 - 200 lit/s)
4. Source Shallow aquifer Deep / Confined aquifers
5. Storage Large storage capacity in the well itself. No storage; draws directly from aquifer.

Types of Tube Wells

(2072 Ashwin Q10, 1+2 Marks)

1. Strainer Type

Most Common

Uses a mesh or slotted screen (strainer) placed against the water-bearing stratum. It prevents sand entry while allowing water.

Suitability: General aquifers (sand/gravel).

2. Cavity Type

No Screen

Draws water from the bottom through a cavity formed in the aquifer below a strong clay layer. No strainer is used.

Suitability: Only when a strong roof (hard clay) exists above the aquifer.

3. Slotted Type

Deep Wells

Uses a slotted steel pipe penetrating rock layers. Often used in borewells where collapse isn't an issue.

Suitability: Rocky formations.

The Recuperation Test

Concept

This test is performed on Open Wells to determine the yield.

  1. Water is pumped out rapidly to create a depression head ($H_1$).
  2. Pumping stops.
  3. The water level starts rising (recuperating) from the aquifer.
  4. We measure the time ($T$) taken for the water level to rise to a new head ($H_2$).
Objective: To find the "Specific Yield per unit Area" ($K/A$ or $C'$).

Essential Formulas

1. Specific Capacity (K/A)

Derived from integration of $dQ = KA h dt$.

$$ \frac{K}{A} = \frac{2.303}{T} \log_{10} \left( \frac{H_1}{H_2} \right) $$
  • $H_1$: Initial Depression Head (m)
  • $H_2$: Final Depression Head (m) after time T
  • $T$: Time in hours (or minutes, just be consistent)
  • $K/A$: Specific yield per unit area ($hr^{-1}$ or $min^{-1}$)

2. Yield of the Well (Q)

Once $K/A$ is known, we calculate discharge for a new diameter/head.

$$ Q = \left( \frac{K}{A} \right) \cdot A_{well} \cdot H $$
  • $A_{well} = \frac{\pi}{4} d^2$ (Cross-sectional area of well)
  • $H$: Working Depression Head

Solution: 2078 Chaitra
2078 Chaitra Q7 [6 Marks]

"For the irrigation project planned in Dhangadi by groundwater pumping, a recuperation test was conducted on an open well. The water level in the well was depressed by 4 m and it was left to raise by 2.2 m in 90 minutes. Calculate the specific yield of open well in that region. Also determine the yield from a well of 5 m diameter under a depression head of 2.5 m?"

Given Data:

$H_1 = 4.0 \text{ m}$
Recup. = $2.2 \text{ m}$
$H_2 = 1.8 \text{ m}$
$T = 1.5 \text{ hrs}$
Static

Step 1: Calculate Specific Yield ($K/A$)

$$ \frac{K}{A} = \frac{2.303}{T} \log_{10} \left( \frac{H_1}{H_2} \right) $$

$$ \frac{K}{A} = \frac{2.303}{1.5} \log_{10} \left( \frac{4.0}{1.8} \right) $$

$$ \frac{K}{A} = 1.535 \times \log_{10}(2.22) = 1.535 \times 0.346 $$

$$ \frac{K}{A} = 0.531 \ hr^{-1} $$

Step 2: Determine Yield ($Q$)

For $d = 5 \text{ m}$ and $H = 2.5 \text{ m}$

Area ($A$) = $\frac{\pi}{4} (5)^2 = 19.635 \text{ m}^2$

$$ Q = \left( \frac{K}{A} \right) \cdot A \cdot H $$

$$ Q = 0.531 \times 19.635 \times 2.5 $$

$$ Q = 26.06 \text{ m}^3/hr $$

($\approx 7.24 \text{ lit/sec}$)

Solution: 2073 & 2071 Bhadra
2073 Q7b / 2071 Q10 [10 Marks]

"During a recuperation test conducted on an open well in a region, the water level in the well was depressed by 3 m and it was observed to rise by 1.75 m in 75 minutes."

  • What is the specific yield of open wells in that region?
  • What could be the yield from a well of 5 m diameter under a depression head of 2.5 m?
  • What should be the diameter of the well to give a yield of 12 lit/s under a depression head of 2 m?

Given Data:

$H_1 = 3.0 \text{ m}$
Rise = $1.75 \text{ m}$
$H_2 = 1.25 \text{ m}$
$T = 1.25 \text{ hrs}$
Static
H1
H2
▲

(i) Specific Yield ($K/A$)

$$ \frac{K}{A} = \frac{2.303}{1.25} \log_{10} \left( \frac{3.0}{1.25} \right) $$

$$ \frac{K}{A} = 1.842 \times \log_{10}(2.4) = 1.842 \times 0.380 $$

$$ \frac{K}{A} = 0.700 \ hr^{-1} $$

(ii) Yield for $d=5m, H=2.5m$

$A = \frac{\pi}{4}(5)^2 = 19.635 \text{ m}^2$

$$ Q = 0.700 \times 19.635 \times 2.5 $$

$$ Q = 34.36 \text{ m}^3/hr $$

(iii) Diameter for $Q=12 l/s$

$Q = 12 \text{ l/s} = 12 \times 3.6 = 43.2 \text{ m}^3/hr$

$H = 2 \text{ m}$

$$ Q = (K/A) \cdot (\frac{\pi}{4}d^2) \cdot H $$

$$ 43.2 = 0.700 \times (0.7854 \cdot d^2) \times 2 $$

$$ 43.2 = 1.10 \cdot d^2 \implies d^2 = 39.27 $$

$$ d = 6.27 \text{ m} $$

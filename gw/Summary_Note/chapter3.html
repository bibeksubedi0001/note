Formula Required

(Horizontal flow)

1. Continuity equation:

\[ \frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial v}{\partial z} = 0 \]

2. Laplace equation:

\[ K_x \frac{\partial^2 h}{\partial x^2} + K_z \frac{\partial^2 h}{\partial z^2} = 0 \]

When \( K_x = K_z = K \): \( \frac{\partial^2 h}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2 h}{\partial z^2} = 0 \)

3. Seepage discharge from flow net:

\[ q = K H \frac{N_f}{N_d} \]

4. Equivalent Permeability:

\[ K_{eq} = \sqrt{K_x \cdot K_z} \]

5. Unconfined flow (Dupuit):

\[ q = \frac{K(h_1^2 - h_2^2)}{2L} \]

6. Phreatic line:

\[ h = \left[ h_1^2 - (h_1^2 - h_2^2)\frac{x}{L} \right]^{1/2} \]

With rainfall P:

\[ h = \left[ h_1^2 - (h_1^2 - h_2^2)\frac{x}{L} + \frac{P}{K}\, x(L - x) \right]^{1/2} \]

7. Confined flow:

\[ q = -K b \frac{dh}{dx} = \frac{K b (h_1 - h_2)}{L} \]

Numerical – Flow Net Analysis

Q. From flow net analysis of well pumping at 180,000 lpm: Nf = 40, Δh = 8 m, b = 25 m. Compute T and K.

Given:

  • Q = 180,000 l/min
  • Nf = 40 (number of flow tubes)
  • Δh = 8 m (head loss per potential drop)
  • b = 25 m (aquifer thickness)

Solution:

Step 1 — Convert Q to m³/day:

\[ Q = 180{,}000 \;\text{l/min} \times \frac{10^{-3}\;\text{m}^3}{\text{l}} \times 60 \times 24 = 259{,}200\;\text{m}^3\text{/day} \]

Step 2 — Apply flow net relation \( Q = T \times N_f \times \Delta h \) to find T:

\[ T = \frac{Q}{N_f \times \Delta h} = \frac{259{,}200}{40 \times 8} = \boxed{810 \;\text{m}^2\text{/day}} \]

Step 3 — Compute K from transmissivity:

\[ K = \frac{T}{b} = \frac{810}{25} = \boxed{32.4 \;\text{m/day}} \]

Numerical – Unconfined Flow Between Two Rivers

Q. Two rivers 5 km apart. K = 18 m/day, h1 = 20 m, h2 = 11 m. Seepage & phreatic line?

Given: L = 5000 m, K = 18 m/day, h1 = 20 m, h2 = 11 m

Solution:

Step 1 — Apply Dupuit formula for seepage per unit width:

\[ q = \frac{K(h_1^2 - h_2^2)}{2L} = \frac{18 \times (20^2 - 11^2)}{2 \times 5000} \]

Step 2 — Compute h1² − h2²:

= 400 − 121 = 279

Step 3 — Evaluate:

\[ q = \frac{18 \times 279}{10{,}000} = \boxed{0.502 \;\text{m}^2\text{/day/m}} \]

Step 4 — Phreatic line equation. Using \( h^2 = h_1^2 - (h_1^2 - h_2^2)\frac{x}{L} \):

\[ h = \sqrt{400 - \frac{279}{5000}\, x} = \sqrt{400 - 0.0558\, x} \]

Numerical – Horizontal Gallery (Unconfined Aquifer)

Q. Gallery 200 m long on impervious stratum. h2 = 8 m at 400 m, h1 = 3 m at gallery face. K = 45 m/day. Find inflow and phreatic surface.

Given: Lgallery = 200 m, L = 400 m, h1 = 3 m, h2 = 8 m, K = 45 m/day

Solution:

Step 1 — Seepage per unit width from one side (Dupuit):

\[ q = \frac{K(h_2^2 - h_1^2)}{2L} = \frac{45(64 - 9)}{800} = \frac{45 \times 55}{800} = 3.094\;\text{m}^2/\text{day/m} \]

Step 2 — Total inflow (both sides, gallery length = 200 m):

\[ Q = q \times L_{\text{gallery}} \times 2 = 3.094 \times 200 \times 2 = \boxed{1237.5\;\text{m}^3\text{/day}} \]

Step 3 — Phreatic surface equation (taking x from gallery face):

\[ h = \sqrt{h_1^2 + (h_2^2 - h_1^2)\frac{x}{L}} = \sqrt{9 + 0.1375\, x} \]

Check: x=0: h=3 ✓ ; x=400: h=√64=8 ✓

Numerical – Rainfall Between Parallel Canals

Q. Strip 1200 m wide, P = 12 mm/hr (100% infiltration), h1 = 10 m, h2 = 8 m, K = 10 m/day. Find discharge, phreatic surface, water divide, seepage without rainfall.

Solution:

Derivation: \( q = -\frac{K}{2} \frac{dh^2}{dx} \), continuity with recharge: \( \frac{dq}{dx} = P \)

\[ \frac{d^2 h^2}{dx^2} = -\frac{2P}{K} \]

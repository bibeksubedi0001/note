Numerical 5 – Confined Aquifer Discharge (Constant Thickness)

Q. Two obs. wells 200 m apart, confined aquifer b = 30 m, heads 9 m and 6 m. K = 11 m/day. Find q.

[Diagram: Confined aquifer cross-section, two wells, b=30m, h1=9m, h2=6m, L=200m]

Given: L = 200 m, b = 30 m, h1 = 9 m, h2 = 6 m, K = 11 m/day

Solution:

Step 1 — Hydraulic gradient: i = (h1 − h2)/L = (9 − 6)/200 = 0.015

Step 2 — Discharge per unit width (Darcy):

\[ q = K \cdot b \cdot i = 11 \times 30 \times 0.015 = \boxed{4.95\;\text{m}^2\text{/day/m}} \]

Step 3 — Convert to SI:

\[ q = \frac{4.95}{86400} = \boxed{5.729 \times 10^{-5}\;\text{m}^3\text{/s/m}} \]

Chapter 4 – Well Hydraulics

Important: Dupuit assumptions on Radial flow. Derivations may be asked.

① Steady Radial Flow

(a) Confined aquifer:

\[ Q = \frac{2\pi K b (h_2 - h_1)}{\ln(r_2/r_1)} = \frac{2\pi T (S_1 - S_2)}{\ln(r_2/r_1)} = \frac{2\pi T S_w}{\ln(R/r_w)} \]

(b) Unconfined aquifer:

\[ Q = \frac{\pi K (h_2^2 - h_1^2)}{\ln(r_2/r_1)} = \frac{\pi K (H^2 - h_w^2)}{\ln(R/r_w)} \]

Page 37

Transcription

② Unsteady Radial Flow

(a) Confined:

\[ \frac{\partial^2 h}{\partial r^2} + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial h}{\partial r} = \frac{S}{T}\frac{\partial h}{\partial t} \]

(b) Unconfined:

\[ \frac{\partial^2 h^2}{\partial r^2} + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial h^2}{\partial r} = \frac{2S}{K}\frac{\partial h}{\partial t} \]

③ Theis Solution

\[ s(r,t) = \frac{Q}{4\pi T} W(u) \quad;\quad u = \frac{r^2 S}{4Tt} \]
\[ W(u) = -0.5772 - \ln(u) + u - \frac{u^2}{2 \cdot 2!} + \frac{u^3}{3 \cdot 3!} - \cdots \]

Page 38

Transcription

④ Partially Penetrating Well

[Diagram: Confined aquifer with partially penetrating well. Screen length hp < b. Flow lines bend toward shorter screen.]

P = hp/b (penetration fraction)

Additional drawdown:

\[ \Delta S_w = \frac{Q}{2\pi T} \cdot \frac{(1-P)}{P} \cdot \ln\frac{(1-P)h_p}{r_w} \]

Kozeny’s Equation:

\[ Q_p = Q\left[P\left(1 + 7\sqrt{\frac{r_w}{2Pb}}\right)\cos\frac{\pi P}{2}\right] \]

⑤ Multiple Well System

Two wells (distance a):

\[ Q = \frac{2\pi Kb(H-h_w)}{\ln(R^2/r_w a)} \]

Three wells (equilateral Δ, side a):

\[ Q = \frac{2\pi Kb(H-h_w)}{\ln(R^3/r_w a^2)} \]

Transcription

Four wells (square, side a):

\[ Q = \frac{2\pi Kb(H-h_w)}{\ln\frac{R^4}{\sqrt{2}\,r_w a^3}} \]

Numerical 1 – Confined Well (Find Q)

Q. 30cm dia. well, confined b=25m, Sw=4m, K=45m/day, R=350m.

Given: d=0.30m → rw=0.15m, b=25m, Sw=4m, K=45m/day, R=350m

Solution:

Step 1 — Compute transmissivity:

T = K × b = 45 × 25 = 1125 m²/day = 1125/86400 = 0.013 m²/s

Step 2 — Compute ln(R/rw):

ln(350/0.15) = ln(2333.3) = 7.754

Step 3 — Apply confined well formula:

\[ Q = \frac{2\pi T S_w}{\ln(R/r_w)} = \frac{2\pi \times 0.013 \times 4}{7.754} = \frac{0.3267}{7.754} = \boxed{0.042\;\text{m}^3\text{/s}} \]

Numerical 7 – % Increase in Discharge

Q. 20cm well, confined b=35m, Sw=2.5m, K=30m/day, R=300m. Q=? Also % increase if d becomes 1.5× larger.

Solution:

T = 30×35 = 1050 m²/day

Case 1: d=20cm, rw=0.10m

\[ Q_1 = \frac{2\pi \times 1050 \times 2.5}{\ln(3000)} = \frac{16493}{8.006} = 2060 \text{ m}^3\text{/day} \]

Case 2: d=30cm, rw=0.15m

\[ Q_2 = \frac{16493}{\ln(2000)} = \frac{16493}{7.601} = 2170 \text{ m}^3\text{/day} \]
\[ \% \text{ increase} = \frac{2170-2060}{2060} \times 100 = \boxed{5.33\%} \]

Note: 50% increase in diameter gives only ~5% increase in Q due to logarithmic relationship.

Numerical 9 – Theis / Well Function

Q. Confined b=30m, Q=2000 l/min, K=25m/day, r=75m, t=10hr, S=0.003. Drawdown=?

Given: b=30m, Q=2000 l/min, K=25 m/day, r=75m, t=10 hr, S=0.003

Solution:

Step 1 — Compute transmissivity:

T = K × b = 25 × 30 = 750 m²/day

Step 2 — Convert T to m²/s: T = 750/86400 = 8.68 × 10−3 m²/s

Step 3 — Convert t to days: t = 10/24 day

Step 4 — Compute u:

\[ u = \frac{r^2 S}{4Tt} = \frac{75^2 \times 0.003}{4 \times 750 \times (10/24)} = \frac{5625 \times 0.003}{1250} = \frac{16.875}{1250} = 0.0135 \]

Step 5 — Compute W(u) using the series:

\[ W(u) = -0.5772 - \ln(0.0135) + 0.0135 - \cdots \]

= −0.5772 + 4.3058 + 0.0135 = 3.741

Step 6 — Convert Q: Q = 2000/(60×1000) = 0.03333 m³/s

Step 7 — Compute drawdown:

\[ s = \frac{Q}{4\pi T} W(u) = \frac{0.03333}{4\pi \times 8.68 \times 10^{-3}} \times 3.741 = 0.3056 \times 3.741 = \boxed{1.143\;\text{m}} \]

Numerical 10 – Partial Penetration (Kozeny)

Q. 40cm well, b=40m, screen 15m, full-pen Q=4.5 m³/min, Sw=4m. Partial-pen yield?

Solution:

P = 15/40 = 0.375; Q = 0.075 m³/s; rw=0.2m

\[ \frac{r_w}{2l_s} = \frac{0.2}{30} = 0.00667 \quad;\quad \sqrt{0.00667} = 0.0816 \]
\[ \frac{\pi P}{2} = 0.5890 \text{ rad} \quad;\quad \cos(0.5890) = 0.8315 \]
\[ \frac{Q_p}{Q} = 0.375 \times [1 + 7 \times 0.0816 \times 0.8315] = 0.375 \times 1.4752 = 0.553 \]
\[ Q_p = 0.075 \times 0.553 = \boxed{0.041 \text{ m}^3\text{/s}} \]

Margin Notes – Cooper–Jacob Method Steps:

  1. Plot semi-log graph: s (linear) vs r (log)
  2. Draw best-fit straight line
  3. Extend to s=0 → Radius of Influence (r0)
  4. Take 1 log cycle: Δs = s1 − s2
  5. \( T = \frac{Q}{2.72\,\Delta s} \)
  6. \( S = \frac{2.25\,T\,t}{r_0^2} \)

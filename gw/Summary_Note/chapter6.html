Formulae

① Specific yield of well (recuperation test):

\[ C = S_y = \frac{1}{T}\ln\left(\frac{h_1}{h_2}\right) \]

② Safe Yield:

\[ Q = C \cdot A \cdot H \]

③ Diameter of well:

\[ d = \sqrt{\frac{4A}{\pi}} \]

Dupuit formula:

\[ Q = 2.72\,K\,L \times \frac{S_w}{\log_{10}(R/r_w)} \]

Numerical 1 – Recuperation Test (Open Well)

Q. Water depressed 3m, rose 1.75m in 75 min. a) Sy? b) Yield from 5m dia. well, H=2.5m? c) Diameter for Q=12 l/s, H=2m?

Solution:

Step 1 — Identify h1 and h2 (depths below SWL):

  • h1 = 3 m (initial depression below SWL)
  • Water rose 1.75 m → h2 = 3 − 1.75 = 1.25 m

T = 75 min = 75/60 = 1.25 hr

a) Specific yield of well:

\[ C = S_y = \frac{1}{T}\ln\left(\frac{h_1}{h_2}\right) = \frac{1}{1.25}\ln\left(\frac{3}{1.25}\right) = 0.8 \times 0.8755 = \boxed{0.7\;\text{per hr}} \]

Page 62

Transcription

b) Yield from well (d=5 m, H=2.5 m):

Step 2 — Apply yield formula Q = C · A · H:

  • Cross-sectional area A = πd²/4 = π×5²/4 = 19.635 m²
  • Depression head H = 2.5 m (working head under pumping)
\[ Q = C \cdot A \cdot H = 0.7 \times 19.635 \times 2.5 = \boxed{34.36 \text{ m}^3\text{/hr}} \]

c) Diameter for Q=12 l/s, H=2 m:

Step 3 — Rearrange for area then diameter:

  • Q = 12 l/s = 12×3600/1000 = 43.2 m³/hr
  • A = Q/(C×H) = 43.2/(0.7×2) = 30.86 m²
\[ d = \sqrt{\frac{4A}{\pi}} = \sqrt{\frac{4 \times 30.86}{\pi}} = \boxed{6.27 \text{ m}} \]

Numerical 2 – Tubewell Design

Q. Q=1300 lpm, confined 12m thick at 25m BGL, SWL=10m, Sw=4m. D10=0.46mm, D30=0.77mm, D50=1.08mm, D60=1.19mm. K=0.1cm/s. Find diameter, strainer length, slot size, gravel pack.

Solution:

Step 1 — Convert units and establish design parameters:

  • Q = 1300 lpm = 1300/(60×1000) = 0.0217 m³/s
  • Entrance velocity Ve = 3 cm/s = 0.03 m/s (recommended for confined)
  • Percent open area P = 15% (strainer open area fraction)
  • K = 0.1 cm/s = 0.001 m/s

Step 2 — From strainer intake formula Q = P·π·D·L·Ve :

\[ Q = P\,\pi\,D\,L\,V_e \quad\Rightarrow\quad 0.0217 = 0.15 \times \pi \times D \times L \times 0.03 \]

⇒ D·L = 1.535 ⇒ L = 1.535/D …(1)

Step 3 — From Dupuit’s formula (R=300 m assumed):

\[ Q = \frac{2.72\,K\,L\,S_w}{\log_{10}\!\left(\frac{R}{r_w}\right)} \quad\Rightarrow\quad 0.0217 = \frac{2.72 \times 0.001 \times L \times 4}{\log_{10}(300/(D/2))} \]

Step 4 — Substitute L = 1.535/D from Eq.(1) and solve simultaneously:

  • Trial & error or graphical: intersection gives
\[ D = \boxed{0.225 \text{ m}} \quad;\quad L = 1.535/0.225 \approx \boxed{7 \text{ m}} \]

Step 5 — Check if natural gravel pack or artificial needed:

\[ C_u = \frac{D_{60}}{D_{10}} = \frac{1.19}{0.46} = 2.6 < 3 \quad\Rightarrow\quad \text{Uniform aquifer} \Rightarrow \text{artificial gravel pack needed} \]

Rule: If Cu < 3, aquifer is too uniform — needs artificial gravel pack to prevent sand entry.

Step 6 — Design the gravel pack:

  • Pack-aquifer ratio = 5: D50(gravel) = 5 × D50(aquifer) = 5 × 1.08 = 5.4 mm
  • Cu of gravel pack ≤ 2.5: D10(pack) = 5.4/2.5 = 2.16 mm
  • Slot size = D10(pack) = 2.16 mm

Numerical 3 – Radial Collector Well

Q. Q=18,000 m³/day, effective radius=70m, lateral dia.=20cm, 15% open area, 40% clogging, Ve=6mm/s. No. of laterals?

Solution:

Step 1 — Convert given data:

  • Q = 18,000 m³/day = 18000/86400 = 0.208 m³/s
  • Ve = 6 mm/s = 0.006 m/s
  • Lateral diameter D = 20 cm = 0.2 m

Step 2 — Find effective screen length (intake from inner 80% only):

Ls = Reff / 0.8 = 70/0.8 = 87.5 m per lateral

Step 3 — Compute number of laterals (accounting for 40% clogging):

\[ n = \frac{Q}{P \times (1-\text{clogging}) \times \pi D \times L_s \times V_e} = \frac{0.208}{0.15 \times 0.60 \times \pi \times 0.2 \times 87.5 \times 0.006} \approx \boxed{11 \text{ laterals}} \]

Note: The factor (1−0.40) = 0.60 accounts for 40% clogging of the strainer open area.

Chapter 8 – Pumps for Groundwater Lifting

(Important for exam)

Types of Pumps (5):

  1. Plunger pump (hand pump)
  2. Jet pump
  3. Centrifugal pump
  4. Submersible pump
  5. Air lift pump

Factors Affecting Selection (6):

  1. Finished internal dia. & total depth of well
  2. Yield from well, desired pumping rate, hours of pumping per day
  3. Lowest pumping water level
  4. Total head on pump
  5. Power required
  6. Quality of water

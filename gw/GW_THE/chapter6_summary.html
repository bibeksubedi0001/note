1. Definition & Objectives Asked 4/5 exams

Groundwater exploration = systematic investigation of subsurface conditions to locate, evaluate, and develop groundwater resources using geological, hydrological, and geophysical techniques.

Five Objectives (write all five in exam)

  1. Determine GW Occurrence — aquifer type (confined/unconfined), location, extent, geological formations
  2. Assess Quantity — well yield, transmissivity (T), storativity (S), recharge potential, safe yield
  3. Evaluate Quality — chemical composition, suitability for drinking/irrigation/industrial, contamination pathways
  4. Select Well Sites — most productive zones, optimal depth & design, minimize drilling cost
  5. GW Management — monitoring plan, water level trends, artificial recharge planning

3. Electrical Resistivity Method Asked 5/5 exams

3.1 Principle

Current (I) injected via two current electrodes; potential difference (ΔV) measured between two potential electrodes. 4-electrode setup eliminates contact resistance error.

\[ \rho_a = K \cdot \frac{\Delta V}{I} \]

$K$ = geometric factor (depends on electrode arrangement); $\rho_a$ = apparent resistivity (Ω·m).

3.2 Typical Resistivity Values

Materialρ (Ω·m)Significance
Clay1–100Low; aquitard
Sand (saturated)50–500Moderate; good aquifer
Sand (dry)500–5000High; unsaturated
Gravel (saturated)100–1000Good aquifer
Crystalline rock1000–100,000+Very high; poor unless fractured
Saline water<1Salt water intrusion indicator

3.3 Electrode Arrays

ArraySetupGeometric Factor K
WennerA–M–N–B at equal spacing 'a'$K = 2\pi a$
SchlumbergerAB/2 = L, MN/2 = l, L ≥ 5l$K \approx \frac{\pi L^2}{2l}$

3.4 VES vs. Horizontal Profiling

VES (Vertical Electrical Sounding)Horizontal Profiling (CST)
CenterFixedMoves along traverse
SpacingProgressively increasesConstant
MeasuresResistivity vs. depthResistivity vs. lateral position
Plotρa vs. a (or AB/2) on log-logρa along traverse line

3.5 VES Curve Types (3-Layer Earth)

TypeResistivity RelationShapeExample
H$\rho_1 > \rho_2 < \rho_3$Bowl (U)Dry soil / saturated sand / bedrock
K$\rho_1 < \rho_2 > \rho_3$Bell (∩)Clay / hard rock / clay
A$\rho_1 < \rho_2 < \rho_3$AscendingClay / sand / rock
Q$\rho_1 > \rho_2 > \rho_3$DescendingDry sand / clay / saline water

Exam tip: In Terai/KTM alluvial settings, aquifer appears as H-type curve ("Sandwich Model") — conductive layer between resistive layers.

3.6 Advantages & Limitations

Advantages:
  • Most widely used for GW
  • Simple, low-cost equipment
  • Depth + lateral info
  • Distinguishes fresh vs. saline
  • Non-destructive
Limitations:
  • Needs flat, open terrain
  • Non-unique interpretation
  • Affected by pipes, power lines
  • Depth limited by spacing
  • Needs borehole calibration

4. Seismic Refraction Method Asked 2/5 exams

4.1 Principle

Seismic wave (hammer/explosion) travels through subsurface; at layer boundary, part refracts along interface and returns to surface. Travel times measured at geophones at known distances.

4.2 Snell's Law & Critical Angle

\[ \frac{\sin i_1}{V_1} = \frac{\sin i_2}{V_2} \qquad \sin i_c = \frac{V_1}{V_2} \quad (V_2 > V_1) \]

4.3 Typical Seismic Velocities

MaterialV (m/s)
Dry soil / loose sand200–600
Saturated alluvium600–1800
Water1450–1530
Sandstone2000–4500
Granite / Gneiss4500–6500

4.4 Time-Distance Graph

  • First segment: direct wave, slope = 1/V1
  • Second segment: refracted wave, slope = 1/V2 (flatter)
  • Crossover distance (xc): where refracted arrives same time as direct
  • Intercept time (ti): refracted line extended to time axis

4.5 Depth Formulas (Two-Layer)

MethodFormula
Crossover distance$h = \dfrac{x_c}{2}\sqrt{\dfrac{V_2 - V_1}{V_2 + V_1}}$
Intercept time$h = \dfrac{t_i \cdot V_1 \cdot V_2}{2\sqrt{V_2^2 - V_1^2}}$

Water table detection: If V2 ≈ 1500 m/s, the interface = water table (P-wave velocity in saturated soil ≈ 1400–1600 m/s).

4.6 Advantages & Limitations

Advantages:
  • Accurate depth to bedrock/WT
  • Rapid field procedure
  • Covers large areas
Limitations:
  • Requires V2 > V1
  • Hidden layer (velocity inversion) → depth overestimated
  • Blind zone (thin layer) → depth underestimated
  • Survey line ≈ 5× target depth

6. Water Witching 2072 Ashwin 1 mark

  • What: Traditional non-scientific method; diviner walks with Y-shaped forked stick or L-shaped rods; device "reacts" over underground water
  • Scientific basis: None. Movement = ideomotor effect (involuntary muscle movements). Success rate = random chance
  • Why it persists: Low cost, simple, fast; in alluvial areas shallow GW is widespread so random drilling often succeeds
  • Key statement: Should never be relied upon for engineering projects; scientific methods always preferred

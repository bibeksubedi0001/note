        <ul>
            <li><strong>Fractured rock aquifers:</strong> Water stored in joints, fractures, and faults. Limited yield (0.5&ndash;5 m&sup3;/hr).</li>
            <li><strong>Colluvial deposits:</strong> Weathered rock and debris on slopes &mdash; seasonal springs.</li>
            <li><strong>River terraces:</strong> Alluvial deposits along river valleys &mdash; moderate yields for dug wells.</li>
        </ul>

        <p>The primary water source in the Middle Hills is <strong>springs</strong>. Nepal has an estimated 6,000+ springs, many of which are declining due to deforestation, climate change, and land-use changes.</p>

        <h3>9.4.2 High Himalayas</h3>
        <ul>
            <li>Crystalline rocks (granite, gneiss) with very low permeability</li>
            <li>Groundwater is negligible except in glacial moraines and talus deposits</li>
            <li>Springs are fed by snowmelt and glacial melt &mdash; highly seasonal</li>
            <li>Not a focus for groundwater development</li>
        </ul>

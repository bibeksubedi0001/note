Question 1 — Equilateral CST with self-weight and edge traction

2080 Chaitra, Q10, Group B
Plane stress, $E = 210 \times 10^3$ MPa, $\nu = 0.3$, $t = 1$ cm, $\gamma = 78.5$ kN/m$^3$, equilateral triangle with side $100$ mm, and edge traction $10$ kN/m$^2$ on face 2-3. Nodes 1 and 2 are restrained.

Figure Q1 — Equilateral CST (100 mm side), pin-supported at nodes 1 & 2, with 10 kN/m² traction on edge 2–3

Solution

Step 1 — Node Coordinates & Given Data

Assign coordinates (convert mm to metres for consistent units):

$$\text{Node 1: }(-0.05,\;0)\;\text{m}, \quad \text{Node 2: }(0.05,\;0)\;\text{m}, \quad \text{Node 3: }(0,\;0.09)\;\text{m}$$

Step 2 — Area of the Triangular Element

Using the determinant formula:

$$2A = \begin{vmatrix}1 & -0.05 & 0 \\ 1 & 0.05 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0.09\end{vmatrix}$$ $$= -0.05(0-0.09) + 0.05(0.09-0) + 0(0-0)$$ $$= 0.00866\;\text{m}^2$$
$$\boxed{A = 0.00433\;\text{m}^2}$$

Step 3 — Coefficients $b_i$ and $c_i$

These are computed from cyclic differences of node coordinates:

$$b_1 = y_2 - y_3 = 0 - 0.09 = -0.09$$ $$b_2 = y_3 - y_1 = 0.09 - 0 = 0.09$$ $$b_3 = y_1 - y_2 = 0 - 0 = 0$$
$$c_1 = x_3 - x_2 = 0 - 0.05 = -0.05$$ $$c_2 = x_1 - x_3 = -0.05 - 0 = -0.05$$ $$c_3 = x_2 - x_1 = 0.05 - -0.05 = 0.1$$
Check: $b_1+b_2+b_3 = 0$ and $c_1+c_2+c_3 = 0$ ✓ (must always be zero)

Step 4 — Strain-Displacement Matrix $[B]$

Using the standard CST formula:

$$[B] = \frac{1}{2A} \begin{bmatrix} b_1 & 0 & b_2 & 0 & b_3 & 0 \\ 0 & c_1 & 0 & c_2 & 0 & c_3 \\ c_1 & b_1 & c_2 & b_2 & c_3 & b_3 \end{bmatrix}$$

Substituting the values:

$$[B] = \begin{bmatrix}-10.00 & 0.00 & 10.00 & 0.00 & 0.00 & 0.00 \\ 0.00 & -5.77 & 0.00 & -5.77 & 0.00 & 11.55 \\ -5.77 & -10.00 & -5.77 & 10.00 & 11.55 & 0.00\end{bmatrix}$$

Step 5 — Constitutive (Material) Matrix $[D]$

For plane stress:

$$[D] = \frac{E}{1-\nu^2} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & \nu & 0 \\ \nu & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{1-\nu}{2} \end{bmatrix}$$
$$[D] = \begin{bmatrix}230769230.77 & 69230769.23 & 0.00 \\ 69230769.23 & 230769230.77 & 0.00 \\ 0.00 & 0.00 & 80769230.77\end{bmatrix}$$

Step 6 — Element Stiffness Matrix $[K]$

Using the formula $[K] = t \cdot A \cdot [B]^T [D] [B]$:

$$[K] = \begin{bmatrix}1115814.53 & 375000.00 & -882647.01 & -28846.15 & -233167.53 & -346153.85 \\ 375000.00 & 682827.23 & 28846.15 & -16634.30 & -403846.15 & -666192.93 \\ -882647.01 & 28846.15 & 1115814.53 & -375000.00 & -233167.53 & 346153.85 \\ -28846.15 & -16634.30 & -375000.00 & 682827.23 & 403846.15 & -666192.93 \\ -233167.53 & -403846.15 & -233167.53 & 403846.15 & 466335.05 & 0.00 \\ -346153.85 & -666192.93 & 346153.85 & -666192.93 & 0.00 & 1332385.86\end{bmatrix}$$

(Units: force/m — stiffness values in the consistent unit system)

Step 7 — Load Vectors

(a) Body force (self-weight in $-y$ direction):

$$\{f_b\} = \frac{\gamma A t}{3} \begin{Bmatrix} 0\\-1\\0\\-1\\0\\-1 \end{Bmatrix} = \frac{78.5 \times 0.00433 \times 0.01}{3} \begin{Bmatrix} 0\\-1\\0\\-1\\0\\-1 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix}0 \\ -1.1330 \times 10^{-3} \\ 0 \\ -1.1330 \times 10^{-3} \\ 0 \\ -1.1330 \times 10^{-3}\end{Bmatrix}\;\text{kN}$$

(b) Traction on edge 2–3: The edge length is $l_{23} = 0.1$ m. The outward normal to edge 2–3 has direction $(0.87, 0.5)$, so the traction components are:

$$t_x = 10 \times 0.87 = 8.66\;\text{kN/m}^2, \quad t_y = 10 \times 0.5 = 5\;\text{kN/m}^2$$
$$\{f_t\}_{23} = \frac{t \cdot l_{23}}{2} \begin{Bmatrix} 0\\0\\t_x\\t_y\\t_x\\t_y \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix}0 \\ 0 \\ 4.3300 \times 10^{-3} \\ 2.5000 \times 10^{-3} \\ 4.3300 \times 10^{-3} \\ 2.5000 \times 10^{-3}\end{Bmatrix}\;\text{kN}$$

(c) Combined load vector:

$$\{F\} = \{f_b\} + \{f_t\} = \begin{Bmatrix}0 \\ -1.1330 \times 10^{-3} \\ 4.3300 \times 10^{-3} \\ 1.3670 \times 10^{-3} \\ 4.3300 \times 10^{-3} \\ 1.3670 \times 10^{-3}\end{Bmatrix}\;\text{kN}$$

Step 8 — Apply Boundary Conditions

Nodes 1 and 2 are restrained (pinned): $u_1 = v_1 = u_2 = v_2 = 0$.

Free DOFs: $u_3$ (DOF 5) and $v_3$ (DOF 6). Extract the $2 \times 2$ sub-system.

Step 9 — Solve for Unknown Displacements

Extract the reduced $2 \times 2$ system:

$$[K]_{ff} = \begin{bmatrix}466335.05 & 0.00 \\ 0.00 & 1332385.86\end{bmatrix}$$
$$\{F\}_{f} = \begin{Bmatrix}4.3300 \times 10^{-3} \\ 1.3670 \times 10^{-3}\end{Bmatrix}$$

Solving $[K]_{ff}\{u\}_f = \{F\}_f$:

$$\boxed{u_3 = 9.2852 \times 10^{-9}\;\text{m}, \qquad v_3 = 1.0260 \times 10^{-9}\;\text{m}}$$

Step 10 — Compute Element Strains and Stresses

Strain (constant throughout the element):

$$\{\varepsilon\} = [B]\{u\} = \begin{Bmatrix} \varepsilon_x \\ \varepsilon_y \\ \gamma_{xy} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ 1.1847 \times 10^{-8} \\ 1.0722 \times 10^{-7} \end{Bmatrix}$$

Stress (constant throughout the element — and equals centroid stress for CST):

$$\boxed{\sigma_x = 0.82\;\text{kN/m²}, \quad \sigma_y = 2.73\;\text{kN/m²}, \quad \tau_{xy} = 8.66\;\text{kN/m²}}$$
For a CST element, strain and stress are constant throughout the element, so the centroid stress is identical to the stress computed above.

Question 5 — Element stiffness matrix and traction load vector

2078 Chaitra, Q6, Group B
Steel plate of thickness $10$ mm with a uniform traction of $5$ kN/m$^2$ on the left edge. Determine the CST element stiffness matrix, load vector, and nodal displacements.

Figure Q5 — Right-triangle CST (free element) with 5 kN/m² traction on edge 1–2 (left vertical edge)

Solution

Step 1 — Node Coordinates & Given Data

Assign coordinates (convert mm to metres for consistent units):

$$\text{Node 1: }(0,\;0)\;\text{m}, \quad \text{Node 2: }(0,\;0.5)\;\text{m}, \quad \text{Node 3: }(0.5,\;0.5)\;\text{m}$$

Step 2 — Area of the Triangular Element

Using the determinant formula:

$$2A = \begin{vmatrix}1 & 0 & 0 \\ 1 & 0 & 0.5 \\ 1 & 0.5 & 0.5\end{vmatrix}$$ $$= 0(0.5-0.5) + 0(0.5-0) + 0.5(0-0.5)$$ $$= -0.25\;\text{m}^2$$
$$\boxed{A = 0.12\;\text{m}^2}$$

Step 3 — Coefficients $b_i$ and $c_i$

These are computed from cyclic differences of node coordinates:

$$b_1 = y_2 - y_3 = 0.5 - 0.5 = 0$$ $$b_2 = y_3 - y_1 = 0.5 - 0 = 0.5$$ $$b_3 = y_1 - y_2 = 0 - 0.5 = -0.5$$
$$c_1 = x_3 - x_2 = 0.5 - 0 = 0.5$$ $$c_2 = x_1 - x_3 = 0 - 0.5 = -0.5$$ $$c_3 = x_2 - x_1 = 0 - 0 = 0$$
Check: $b_1+b_2+b_3 = 0$ and $c_1+c_2+c_3 = 0$ ✓ (must always be zero)

Step 4 — Strain-Displacement Matrix $[B]$

Using the standard CST formula:

$$[B] = \frac{1}{2A} \begin{bmatrix} b_1 & 0 & b_2 & 0 & b_3 & 0 \\ 0 & c_1 & 0 & c_2 & 0 & c_3 \\ c_1 & b_1 & c_2 & b_2 & c_3 & b_3 \end{bmatrix}$$

Substituting the values:

$$[B] = \begin{bmatrix}-0.00 & -0.00 & -2.00 & -0.00 & 2.00 & -0.00 \\ -0.00 & -2.00 & -0.00 & 2.00 & -0.00 & -0.00 \\ -2.00 & -0.00 & 2.00 & -2.00 & -0.00 & 2.00\end{bmatrix}$$

Step 5 — Constitutive (Material) Matrix $[D]$

For plane stress:

$$[D] = \frac{E}{1-\nu^2} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & \nu & 0 \\ \nu & 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & \frac{1-\nu}{2} \end{bmatrix}$$
$$[D] = \begin{bmatrix}219780219.78 & 65934065.93 & 0.00 \\ 65934065.93 & 219780219.78 & 0.00 \\ 0.00 & 0.00 & 76923076.92\end{bmatrix}$$

Step 6 — Element Stiffness Matrix $[K]$

Using the formula $[K] = t \cdot A \cdot [B]^T [D] [B]$:

$$[K] = \begin{bmatrix}384615.38 & 0.00 & -384615.38 & 384615.38 & 0.00 & -384615.38 \\ 0.00 & 1098901.10 & 329670.33 & -1098901.10 & -329670.33 & 0.00 \\ -384615.38 & 329670.33 & 1483516.48 & -714285.71 & -1098901.10 & 384615.38 \\ 384615.38 & -1098901.10 & -714285.71 & 1483516.48 & 329670.33 & -384615.38 \\ 0.00 & -329670.33 & -1098901.10 & 329670.33 & 1098901.10 & 0.00 \\ -384615.38 & 0.00 & 384615.38 & -384615.38 & 0.00 & 384615.38\end{bmatrix}$$

(Units: force/m — stiffness values in the consistent unit system)

Step 7 — Load Vector

Traction of $5$ kN/m² acts in the $+x$ direction on edge 1–2 (left vertical edge). Edge length $l_{12} = 0.5$ m.

$$\{F\} = \frac{t \cdot l_{12}}{2} \begin{Bmatrix} t_x \\ t_y \\ t_x \\ t_y \\ 0 \\ 0 \end{Bmatrix} = \frac{0.01 \times 0.5}{2} \begin{Bmatrix} 5\\0\\5\\0\\0\\0 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix}1.2500 \times 10^{-2} \\ 0 \\ 1.2500 \times 10^{-2} \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0\end{Bmatrix}\;\text{kN}$$

Step 8 — Apply Boundary Conditions

No support conditions are given — this is a free (unsupported) element.

Step 9 — Solve for Unknown Displacements

Since no boundary conditions are imposed, the stiffness matrix is singular (rank 3). A unique displacement solution cannot be obtained from the free element alone.

Important: The element stiffness matrix [K] and load vector {F} are well-defined. However, without support conditions, the system has 3 rigid-body modes, making [K] singular. To obtain displacements, support conditions must first be specified.

Step 10 — Compute Element Strains and Stresses

Without support conditions, strains and stresses cannot be computed for the free element.

Important: A free single CST element has rank 3 (3 rigid-body modes: 2 translations + 1 rotation). The $6 \times 6$ stiffness matrix is singular. To solve for displacements, at least 3 independent support conditions must be imposed. The stiffness matrix $[K]$ and load vector $\{F\}$ computed above are the required answers.

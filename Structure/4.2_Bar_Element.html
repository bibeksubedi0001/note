Stiffness Matrix & Force-Displacement Relationship

→ Stiffness of a spring element is defined as the load per unit displacement. i.e.

$$k = \frac{P}{\delta} \quad \Rightarrow \quad P = k \cdot \delta$$

→ Similarly, for a bar element, we know,

$$\delta = \frac{Pl}{AE} \quad \Rightarrow \quad \frac{P}{\delta} = \frac{AE}{l} = k \;\text{ (Stiffness)}$$
$$P = k \cdot \delta = \frac{AE}{l} \cdot \delta$$

Considering a bar element or spring element acted upon by forces $F_1$ & $F_2$ at nodes 1 & 2 respectively. Let $u_1$ & $u_2$ be the displacements at nodes 1 & 2 respectively.

Fig: Force and displacement acting at corresponding nodes of a bar/spring element

Hence, net elongation, $\delta = u_2 - u_1$

Also, for equilibrium:

$$F_1 + F_2 = 0 \quad \Rightarrow \quad F_1 = -F_2 \quad \Rightarrow \quad F_2 = -F_1$$

Now,

$$F_2 = k \cdot \delta = k(u_2 - u_1) = -ku_1 + ku_2$$
$$F_1 = -k(u_2 - u_1) = ku_1 - ku_2$$

Hence, in matrix form:

$$\begin{Bmatrix} F_1 \\ F_2 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} k & -k \\ -k & k \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}$$

or, $\{F\} = [K]\{u\}$

where,

  • $\{F\}$ = Force vector
  • $[K]$ = Stiffness matrix for bar/spring element ($k = \frac{AE}{l}$ for bar element)
  • $\{u\}$ = Displacement vector

3-Noded Bar/Spring Element

Fig: 3-noded bar/spring element with two elements

For 1st element:

$$\begin{Bmatrix} f_{11} \\ f_{12} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} k_1 & -k_1 \\ -k_1 & k_1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}$$

For 2nd element:

$$\begin{Bmatrix} f_{22} \\ f_{23} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} k_2 & -k_2 \\ -k_2 & k_2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix}$$

Assembled equation is:

$$\begin{Bmatrix} F_1 \\ F_2 \\ F_3 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} k_1 & -k_1 & 0 \\ -k_1 & k_1+k_2 & -k_2 \\ 0 & -k_2 & k_2 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix}$$

where $F_2 = f_{12} + f_{22}$, and $f_{ij}$ = force in element $i$ at node $j$.

$\therefore \{F\} = [K]\{u\}$

Stress & Strain in an Element

$$\begin{Bmatrix} f_{11} \\ f_{12} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} k & -k \\ -k & k \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}$$
$$f_{11} = k(u_1 - u_2) = -k(u_2 - u_1)$$ $$f_{12} = -k(u_1 - u_2) = k(u_2 - u_1) = \frac{EA}{l}(u_2 - u_1)$$

$\therefore$ Element stress,

$$\sigma = \frac{f_{12}}{A} = \frac{E}{l}(u_2 - u_1) = \frac{E}{l} \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} = E[B]\{u\}$$

and, Strain,

$$\epsilon = \frac{\sigma}{E} = \frac{1}{l} \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} = [B]\{u\}$$

where $[B] = \frac{1}{l} \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$ is the strain-displacement matrix.

Equivalent Nodal Loads

Any type of load acting at any point of the element should be transferred to nodes as equivalent nodal loads in FEM.

i) Body Force

A body force is a force that acts throughout the volume of the body. For example, gravity force.

Let $\gamma$ = unit weight of material of bar, $A$ = cross-sectional area, $l$ = length of bar, $W$ = weight.

Then, $W = \gamma \cdot A \cdot l$

Dividing equally to the two nodes:

$$\begin{Bmatrix} f_{b1} \\ f_{b2} \end{Bmatrix} = \frac{\gamma \cdot A \cdot l}{2} \begin{Bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix}$$

ii) Surface Traction (Surface Load)

If $S_t$ is the intensity of surface load and $P$ be the perimeter, then total load is:

$$f_s = S_t \cdot P \cdot l$$

Dividing the values equally to two nodes:

$$\begin{Bmatrix} f_{s1} \\ f_{s2} \end{Bmatrix} = \frac{S_t \cdot P \cdot l}{2} \begin{Bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix}$$
→ Point loads are directly added to nodal force vectors.

Temperature Effect

We know; change in length due to change in temperature $\Delta t$ is given by:

$$\Delta l = \alpha \cdot l \cdot \Delta t$$
$$\frac{Pl}{AE} = \alpha \cdot l \cdot \Delta t \quad \Rightarrow \quad P = AE \cdot \alpha \cdot \Delta t$$

$\therefore$ Element load vector due to temperature change is given by:

$$\{f_t^e\} = \begin{Bmatrix} f_{1t} \\ f_{2t} \end{Bmatrix} = AE\alpha \cdot \Delta t \begin{Bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix}$$
$$\text{Stress in element} = \frac{E}{l} \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} - E\alpha \cdot \Delta t = E[B]\{u\} - E\alpha \cdot \Delta t$$
$$\text{Strain in element} = [B]\{u\} - \alpha \cdot \Delta t$$

Effect of Lack of Fit

$$\{f_{\delta}\} = \begin{Bmatrix} f_{1\delta} \\ f_{2\delta} \end{Bmatrix} = \frac{AE\delta}{l} \begin{Bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix}$$

($\delta$ is +ve when element is very long & −ve when element is short.)

$$\text{Stress} = \frac{E}{l} \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} - E \cdot \frac{\delta}{l} = E[B]\{u\} - E \cdot \frac{\delta}{l}$$
$$\text{Strain} = \frac{1}{l} \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix} - \frac{\delta}{l} = [B]\{u\} - \frac{\delta}{l}$$

Solved Examples

Example 1
Q. For the spring assemblage with arbitrary numbered nodes shown in figure below, obtain: (a) the global stiffness matrix, (b) displacement of nodes 3 and 4, (c) the reaction forces at nodes 1 and 2, and (d) the forces in each spring. A force of 5000 N is applied at node 4.
Fig: Spring assemblage with arbitrarily numbered nodes
Solution

Force vector:

$$\{f\} = \begin{Bmatrix} f_1 \\ f_2 \\ f_3 \\ f_4 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} R_1 \\ R_2 \\ 0 \\ 5000 \end{Bmatrix}$$

Displacement vector:

$$\{u\} = \begin{Bmatrix} u_1 \\ u_2 \\ u_3 \\ u_4 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ u_3 \\ u_4 \end{Bmatrix}$$

Stiffness matrix:

For element 1 (nodes 1, 3):

$$[K^1] = \begin{bmatrix} 1000 & -1000 \\ -1000 & 1000 \end{bmatrix} \quad \text{(rows/cols: 1, 3)}$$

For element 2 (nodes 2, 4):

$$[K^2] = \begin{bmatrix} 2000 & -2000 \\ -2000 & 2000 \end{bmatrix} \quad \text{(rows/cols: 2, 4)}$$

For element 3 (nodes 3, 4):

$$[K^3] = \begin{bmatrix} 3000 & -3000 \\ -3000 & 3000 \end{bmatrix} \quad \text{(rows/cols: 3, 4)}$$

Now, writing global stiffness matrix by superposition:

$$[K] = 1000 \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 0 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 4 & -3 \\ 0 & -2 & -3 & 5 \end{bmatrix}$$

Force-displacement relationship: $\{f\} = [K]\{u\}$

$$\begin{Bmatrix} R_1 \\ R_2 \\ 0 \\ 5000 \end{Bmatrix} = 1000 \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 0 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 4 & -3 \\ 0 & -2 & -3 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ u_3 \\ u_4 \end{Bmatrix}$$

Eliminating rows and columns associated with $u = 0$ (boundary conditions), i.e., rows/columns 1 & 2 (since $u_1 = 0$ and $u_2 = 0$), and writing reduced matrix equation for unknown DOFs ($u_3$ and $u_4$):

$$\begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ 5000 \end{Bmatrix} = 1000 \begin{bmatrix} 4 & -3 \\ -3 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_3 \\ u_4 \end{Bmatrix}$$

Solving the 2×2 system using matrix inversion formula: $\{u\} = [K_r]^{-1}\{F_r\}$.

For a 2×2 matrix, the inverse is: $\begin{bmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{bmatrix}^{-1} = \frac{1}{ad - bc} \begin{bmatrix} d & -b \\ -c & a \end{bmatrix}$

$$\text{Determinant of } [K_r] = 1000^2 \times (4 \times 5 - (-3)(-3)) = 1000^2 \times (20 - 9) = 11 \times 10^6$$
$$[K_r]^{-1} = \frac{1}{11 \times 10^6} \begin{bmatrix} 5000 & 3000 \\ 3000 & 4000 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{1}{11000} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 3 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix}$$
$$\begin{Bmatrix} u_3 \\ u_4 \end{Bmatrix} = \frac{1}{11000} \begin{bmatrix} 5 & 3 \\ 3 & 4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ 5000 \end{Bmatrix} = \frac{1}{11000} \begin{Bmatrix} 15000 \\ 20000 \end{Bmatrix}$$
$$\therefore \begin{Bmatrix} u_3 \\ u_4 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} 15/11 \\ 20/11 \end{Bmatrix} \approx \begin{Bmatrix} 1.363 \\ 1.818 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ m}$$

(c) Reaction forces: Back-substitute the displacements into the full global equation $\{f\} = [K]\{u\}$:

$$\begin{Bmatrix} R_1 \\ R_2 \\ 0 \\ 5000 \end{Bmatrix} = 1000 \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 & -1 & 0 \\ 0 & 2 & 0 & -2 \\ -1 & 0 & 4 & -3 \\ 0 & -2 & -3 & 5 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 15/11 \\ 20/11 \end{Bmatrix}$$

From Row 1: $R_1 = 1000 \times (1 \times 0 + 0 \times 0 - 1 \times \frac{15}{11} + 0 \times \frac{20}{11})$

$$R_1 = 1000 \times \left(-\frac{15}{11}\right) = -\frac{15000}{11} = -1363.63 \text{ N}$$

From Row 2: $R_2 = 1000 \times (0 + 2 \times 0 + 0 \times \frac{15}{11} - 2 \times \frac{20}{11})$

$$R_2 = 1000 \times \left(-\frac{40}{11}\right) = -\frac{40000}{11} = -3636.36 \text{ N}$$
$$R_1 = -1363.63 \text{ N}, \qquad R_2 = -3636.36 \text{ N}$$

Equilibrium check: $\sum F = R_1 + R_2 + P = -1363.63 - 3636.36 + 5000 \approx 0$   $\checkmark$

Forces in individual springs:

Element 1:

$$\begin{Bmatrix} f_{11} \\ f_{13} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 1000 & -1000 \\ -1000 & 1000 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ 15/11 \end{Bmatrix}$$
$f_{11} = -15000/11 = -1363.63$ N, $\quad f_{13} = +15000/11 = +1363.63$ N (Tensile)

Element 2:

$$\begin{Bmatrix} f_{22} \\ f_{24} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 2000 & -2000 \\ -2000 & 2000 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ 20/11 \end{Bmatrix}$$
$f_{22} = -40000/11 = -3636.36$ N, $\quad f_{24} = +40000/11 = +3636.36$ N (Tensile)

Element 3:

$$\begin{Bmatrix} f_{33} \\ f_{34} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 3000 & -3000 \\ -3000 & 3000 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 15/11 \\ 20/11 \end{Bmatrix}$$
$f_{33} = -15000/11 = -1363.63$ N, $\quad f_{34} = +15000/11 = +1363.63$ N (Tensile)
Example 2
Q. For the spring assemblage shown in figure, obtain: (a) the global stiffness matrix, (b) the displacement of nodes 2, 3 & 4, (c) the global nodal forces, and (d) the local element forces. Node 1 is fixed while node 5 is given a fixed known displacement $\delta = 20\text{ mm}$. The spring constant for all springs are equal to $k = 200\text{ kN/m}$.
Fig: 5-node spring assemblage with known displacement at node 5
Solution

Given: 5 nodes, 4 spring elements (all with $k = 200$ kN/m), $u_1 = 0$ (fixed), $u_5 = 20$ mm $= 0.02$ m (known displacement), no external load on nodes 2, 3, 4.

Element connectivity:

  • Element 1: nodes 1 – 2
  • Element 2: nodes 2 – 3
  • Element 3: nodes 3 – 4
  • Element 4: nodes 4 – 5

Force vector: (Reactions at constrained nodes; no external load at free nodes)

$$\{f\} = \begin{Bmatrix} R_1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ R_5 \end{Bmatrix}$$

Displacement vector: ($u_5 = 20$ mm $= 0.02$ m)

$$\{u\} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ u_2 \\ u_3 \\ u_4 \\ 0.02 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ m}$$

(a) Global Stiffness Matrix:

For each element, the stiffness matrix is given by:

$$[K^i] = k \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 200 & -200 \\ -200 & 200 \end{bmatrix} \text{ kN/m}$$

Element 1 (nodes 1, 2):

$$[K^1] = \begin{bmatrix} 200 & -200 \\ -200 & 200 \end{bmatrix} \quad \text{(rows/cols: 1, 2)}$$

Element 2 (nodes 2, 3):

$$[K^2] = \begin{bmatrix} 200 & -200 \\ -200 & 200 \end{bmatrix} \quad \text{(rows/cols: 2, 3)}$$

Element 3 (nodes 3, 4):

$$[K^3] = \begin{bmatrix} 200 & -200 \\ -200 & 200 \end{bmatrix} \quad \text{(rows/cols: 3, 4)}$$

Element 4 (nodes 4, 5):

$$[K^4] = \begin{bmatrix} 200 & -200 \\ -200 & 200 \end{bmatrix} \quad \text{(rows/cols: 4, 5)}$$

Assembling the $5 \times 5$ global stiffness matrix by direct stiffness method (superimposing element contributions at corresponding DOFs):

$$[K] = \begin{bmatrix} 200 & -200 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ -200 & 400 & -200 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -200 & 400 & -200 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -200 & 400 & -200 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -200 & 200 \end{bmatrix} \text{ kN/m}$$

Note: Diagonal entries at interior nodes are $k + k = 400$, and end nodes have just $k = 200$.

(b) Force-displacement relationship: $\{f\} = [K]\{u\}$

$$\begin{Bmatrix} R_1 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ 0 \\ R_5 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 200 & -200 & 0 & 0 & 0 \\ -200 & 400 & -200 & 0 & 0 \\ 0 & -200 & 400 & -200 & 0 \\ 0 & 0 & -200 & 400 & -200 \\ 0 & 0 & 0 & -200 & 200 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ u_2 \\ u_3 \\ u_4 \\ 0.02 \end{Bmatrix}$$

Eliminating rows/columns for known DOFs ($u_1 = 0$ and $u_5 = 0.02$ m) and writing equations for rows 2, 3, 4:

Row 2: $0 = 400 u_2 - 200 u_3$

Row 3: $0 = -200 u_2 + 400 u_3 - 200 u_4$

Row 4: $0 = -200 u_3 + 400 u_4 - 200 \times 0.02$

The term $-200 \times 0.02 = -4$ kN from the known displacement $u_5$ is moved to the left-hand side:

$$\begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \\ 4 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 400 & -200 & 0 \\ -200 & 400 & -200 \\ 0 & -200 & 400 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_2 \\ u_3 \\ u_4 \end{Bmatrix}$$

Solving the 3×3 system:

From Row 1: $400 u_2 = 200 u_3 \Rightarrow u_2 = 0.5\, u_3$   — (i)

From Row 2: $-200 u_2 + 400 u_3 - 200 u_4 = 0$

Substituting (i): $-200(0.5\,u_3) + 400 u_3 - 200 u_4 = 0 \Rightarrow 300 u_3 = 200 u_4 \Rightarrow u_3 = \frac{2}{3}u_4$   — (ii)

From Row 3: $-200 u_3 + 400 u_4 = 4$

Substituting (ii): $-200 \times \frac{2}{3}u_4 + 400 u_4 = 4 \Rightarrow \frac{800}{3} u_4 = 4 \Rightarrow u_4 = 0.015$ m

Back-substituting: $u_3 = \frac{2}{3} \times 0.015 = 0.01$ m   and   $u_2 = 0.5 \times 0.01 = 0.005$ m

$$\begin{Bmatrix} u_2 \\ u_3 \\ u_4 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0.005 \\ 0.01 \\ 0.015 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ m} = \begin{Bmatrix} 5 \\ 10 \\ 15 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ mm}$$
The displacements vary linearly (5, 10, 15, 20 mm), which is expected since all springs are identical and the only imposed loading is the displacement at node 5.

(c) Global nodal forces (Reactions):

Using the original full equation $\{f\} = [K]\{u\}$ with all displacements now known: $\{u\} = \{0, 0.005, 0.01, 0.015, 0.02\}^T$ m.

Row 1: $R_1 = 200 \times 0 - 200 \times 0.005 = -1$ kN

Row 5: $R_5 = -200 \times 0.015 + 200 \times 0.02 = -3 + 4 = 1$ kN

$$R_1 = -1 \text{ kN}, \qquad R_5 = +1 \text{ kN}$$

Equilibrium check: $R_1 + R_5 = -1 + 1 = 0$   $\checkmark$ (No external loads applied, so net force must be zero.)

(d) Forces in individual springs:

Using $\{f^e\} = [K^e]\{u^e\}$ for each element:

Element 1 (nodes 1–2): $u_1 = 0$, $u_2 = 0.005$ m

$$\begin{Bmatrix} f_{11} \\ f_{12} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 200 & -200 \\ -200 & 200 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ 0.005 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ kN}$$
$f_{11} = -1$ kN, $f_{12} = +1$ kN (Tensile, spring elongates)

Element 2 (nodes 2–3): $u_2 = 0.005$, $u_3 = 0.01$ m

$$\begin{Bmatrix} f_{22} \\ f_{23} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 200 & -200 \\ -200 & 200 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 0.005 \\ 0.01 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ kN}$$
$f_{22} = -1$ kN, $f_{23} = +1$ kN (Tensile)

Element 3 (nodes 3–4): $u_3 = 0.01$, $u_4 = 0.015$ m

$$\begin{Bmatrix} f_{33} \\ f_{34} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 200 & -200 \\ -200 & 200 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 0.01 \\ 0.015 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ kN}$$
$f_{33} = -1$ kN, $f_{34} = +1$ kN (Tensile)

Element 4 (nodes 4–5): $u_4 = 0.015$, $u_5 = 0.02$ m

$$\begin{Bmatrix} f_{44} \\ f_{45} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} 200 & -200 \\ -200 & 200 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 0.015 \\ 0.02 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ kN}$$
$f_{44} = -1$ kN, $f_{45} = +1$ kN (Tensile)
All springs carry the same force of 1 kN (tensile). This is expected because the system is in series with no external loads at intermediate nodes.
Example 3
Q. In the figure given below, a load $P = 60$ kN is applied as shown. Determine the displacement field, stress and support reaction. Take $E = 20 \times 10^3$ N/mm². There is a gap of 1.2 mm between the right end and the wall.
Fig: Bar with load P, fixed at left and gap to wall at right

Given: Two bar elements with $A_1 = A_2 = 250$ mm², $L_1 = L_2 = 150$ mm, $E = 20 \times 10^3$ N/mm². Load $P = 60$ kN at node 2. Gap of 1.2 mm between node 3 and the right wall. Node 1 is fixed ($u_1 = 0$).

Solution

Step 1: Identify unknowns and boundary conditions.

Initially, we assume the gap is not closed. Node 3 is free, so $F_3 = 0$.

Force vector:

$$\{F\} = \begin{Bmatrix} R_1 \\ 60 \times 10^3 \\ 0 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ N}$$

Displacement vector:

$$\{u\} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix}$$

Step 2: Element stiffness matrices.

Using $[K^e] = \frac{AE}{L}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$:

$$k = \frac{AE}{L} = \frac{250 \times 20 \times 10^3}{150} = \frac{5 \times 10^6}{150} = \frac{10^5}{3} = 33{,}333.33 \text{ N/mm}$$
$$[K^1] = [K^2] = \frac{10^5}{3} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$$

Step 3: Global stiffness matrix (assembling by direct stiffness method):

$$[K] = \frac{10^5}{3} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 0 \\ -1 & 2 & -1 \\ 0 & -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$$

Step 4: Apply boundary conditions and solve.

$\{F\} = [K]\{u\}$:

$$\begin{Bmatrix} R_1 \\ 60 \times 10^3 \\ 0 \end{Bmatrix} = \frac{10^5}{3} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 0 \\ -1 & 2 & -1 \\ 0 & -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix}$$

Eliminating row/column 1 (since $u_1 = 0$), the reduced equations are:

$$60{,}000 = \frac{10^5}{3}(2 u_2 - u_3) \quad --- (i)$$ $$0 = \frac{10^5}{3}(-u_2 + u_3) \quad --- (ii)$$

From equation (ii): $u_3 = u_2$. Substituting into (i):

$$60{,}000 = \frac{10^5}{3}(2u_2 - u_2) = \frac{10^5}{3} u_2 \quad \Rightarrow \quad u_2 = \frac{60{,}000 \times 3}{10^5} = 1.8 \text{ mm}$$
$$u_2 = 1.8 \text{ mm}, \qquad u_3 = 1.8 \text{ mm}$$

Step 5: Check if the gap closes.

The displacement at node 3 is $u_3 = 1.8$ mm, but the gap is only 1.2 mm. Since $u_3 > 1.2$ mm, the right end touches the wall and cannot displace further. The wall develops a reaction force $R_3$.

Step 6: Re-solve with gap constraint $u_3 = 1.2$ mm.

$$\{F\} = \begin{Bmatrix} R_1 \\ 60 \times 10^3 \\ R_3 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ N}, \qquad \{u\} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ u_2 \\ 1.2 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ mm}$$

From the global equation, Row 2 gives:

$$60{,}000 = \frac{10^5}{3}(-0 + 2u_2 - 1.2) = \frac{10^5}{3}(2u_2 - 1.2)$$
$$2u_2 - 1.2 = \frac{60{,}000 \times 3}{10^5} = 1.8 \quad \Rightarrow \quad u_2 = \frac{1.8 + 1.2}{2} = 1.5 \text{ mm}$$

Step 7: Back-substitute to find reactions.

Row 1: $R_1 = \frac{10^5}{3}(0 - u_2 + 0) = \frac{10^5}{3}(-1.5)$

$$R_1 = -50{,}000 \text{ N} = -50 \text{ kN}$$

Row 3: $R_3 = \frac{10^5}{3}(0 - u_2 + u_3) = \frac{10^5}{3}(-1.5 + 1.2)$

$$R_3 = \frac{10^5}{3}(-0.3) = -10{,}000 \text{ N} = -10 \text{ kN}$$
$$u_2 = 1.5 \text{ mm}, \quad u_3 = 1.2 \text{ mm}$$ $$R_1 = -50 \text{ kN}, \quad R_3 = -10 \text{ kN}$$

Equilibrium check: $R_1 + P + R_3 = -50 + 60 + (-10) = 0$   $\checkmark$

Step 8: Element stresses.

Using $\sigma = \frac{E}{L}(u_2 - u_1)$ for each element:

Element 1 (nodes 1–2):

$$\sigma_1 = \frac{E}{L}(u_2 - u_1) = \frac{20 \times 10^3}{150}(1.5 - 0) = 133.33 \times 1.5 = 200 \text{ N/mm}^2 \text{ (Tensile)}$$

Element 2 (nodes 2–3):

$$\sigma_2 = \frac{E}{L}(u_3 - u_2) = \frac{20 \times 10^3}{150}(1.2 - 1.5) = 133.33 \times (-0.3) = -40 \text{ N/mm}^2 \text{ (Compressive)}$$
$$\sigma_1 = +200 \text{ N/mm}^2 \text{ (Tension)}, \qquad \sigma_2 = -40 \text{ N/mm}^2 \text{ (Compression)}$$
Example 4
Q. A 4 m long 300 mm × 300 mm concrete member is vertically hanging. Investigate the state of deformation and stress in the member subjected to gravity. Given: analytical displacement $u(x) = \frac{\gamma}{E}\left(Lx - \frac{x^2}{2}\right)$, stress $\sigma(x) = \gamma(L - x)$. Take $E = 2 \times 10^4$ N/mm², $\gamma = 25 \times 10^{-6}$ N/mm³.
Fig: Vertically hanging concrete member
Solution — Using One Element

Force vector:

$$\{F\} = \frac{\gamma \cdot A \cdot l}{2} \begin{Bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix} + \begin{Bmatrix} R_1 \\ 0 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} 4500 + R_1 \\ 4500 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ N}$$

Displacement vector: $\{u\} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ u_2 \end{Bmatrix}$

Stiffness matrix:

$$[K] = \frac{EA}{l} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = \frac{2 \times 10^4 \times 300^2}{4000} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = 4.5 \times 10^5 \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$$

$\{F\} = [K]\{u\}$:

$$4500 = 4.5 \times 10^5 \times u_2$$
$$u_2 = 0.01 \text{ mm}$$

Stress:

$$\sigma = E[B]\{u\} = 2 \times 10^4 \times \frac{1}{4000} \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ 0.01 \end{Bmatrix} = 0.05 \text{ N/mm}^2$$

Verification from analytical relation at $x = 4000$:

$$u(x) = \frac{25 \times 10^{-6}}{2 \times 10^4} \left(4000 \times 4000 - \frac{4000^2}{2}\right) = 0.01 \text{ mm} \quad \checkmark$$

At C.G. of element ($x = 2000$):

$$\sigma(x) = \gamma(L - x) = 25 \times 10^{-6}(4000 - 2000) = 0.05 \text{ N/mm}^2 \quad \checkmark$$
Solution — Using Two Elements

Load vector:

$$\{f_{b1}\} = \frac{\gamma A_1 L_1}{2} \begin{Bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix} = 2250 \begin{Bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix}, \quad \{f_{b2}\} = 2250 \begin{Bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix}$$
$$\{f_b\} = \begin{Bmatrix} 2250 \\ 4500 \\ 2250 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ N}$$
$$\{F\} = \begin{Bmatrix} R_1 + 2250 \\ 4500 \\ 2250 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ N}, \quad \{u\} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix}$$

Stiffness matrix:

$$[K^1] = [K^2] = \frac{2 \times 10^4 \times (300)^2}{2000} \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} = 9 \times 10^5 \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$$
$$[K] = 9 \times 10^5 \begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 & 0 \\ -1 & 2 & -1 \\ 0 & -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$$

Reduced matrix equation:

$$\begin{Bmatrix} 4500 \\ 2250 \end{Bmatrix} = 9 \times 10^5 \begin{bmatrix} 2 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix}$$
$$\begin{Bmatrix} u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0.0075 \\ 0.01 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ mm}$$

Verification: At Node 2 ($x=2000$): $u_2 = 0.0075$ mm $\checkmark$   At Node 3 ($x=4000$): $u_3 = 0.01$ mm $\checkmark$

Stresses:

$$\sigma_1 = \frac{2 \times 10^4}{2000} \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ 0.0075 \end{Bmatrix} = 0.075 \text{ N/mm}^2$$
$$\sigma_2 = \frac{2 \times 10^4}{2000} \begin{bmatrix} -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 0.0075 \\ 0.01 \end{Bmatrix} = 0.025 \text{ N/mm}^2$$

Verified at C.G. of element 1 ($x = 1000$): $\sigma = 0.075$ N/mm² $\checkmark$

Verified at C.G. of element 2 ($x = 3000$): $\sigma = 0.025$ N/mm² $\checkmark$

Example 5
Q. The stepped bar shown in figure is subjected to an increase in temperature $\Delta t = 80°C$. Determine the displacements, stresses and the support reactions.
Fig: Stepped bar with temperature increase and applied loads
Solution

Given data:

PropertyElement 1 (Aluminium)Element 2 (Steel)Element 3 (Bronze)
Length $L$ (mm)800600400
Area $A$ (mm²)24001200600
$E$ (GPa = 10³ N/mm²)8370200
$\alpha$ (/°C)18.9 × 10&supmin;&sup6;23 × 10&supmin;&sup6;11.7 × 10&supmin;&sup6;

$\Delta t = 80$°C, $P_1 = -60$ kN at node 2 (leftward), $P_2 = -75$ kN at node 3 (leftward). Both ends fixed: $u_1 = u_4 = 0$.

Step 1: Element thermal load vectors.

Using $\{f_t^e\} = AE\alpha \Delta t \begin{Bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix}$:

Element 1: $A_1 E_1 \alpha_1 \Delta t = 2400 \times 83 \times 10^3 \times 18.9 \times 10^{-6} \times 80$

$$= 2400 \times 83000 \times 1.512 \times 10^{-3} = 2400 \times 125.496 = 301{,}190.4 \text{ N}$$
$$\{f_t^1\} = 301{,}190.4 \begin{Bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} -301{,}190.4 \\ 301{,}190.4 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ N} \quad \text{(nodes 1, 2)}$$

Element 2: $A_2 E_2 \alpha_2 \Delta t = 1200 \times 70 \times 10^3 \times 23 \times 10^{-6} \times 80$

$$= 1200 \times 70000 \times 1.84 \times 10^{-3} = 1200 \times 128.8 = 154{,}560 \text{ N}$$
$$\{f_t^2\} = 154{,}560 \begin{Bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} -154{,}560 \\ 154{,}560 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ N} \quad \text{(nodes 2, 3)}$$

Element 3: $A_3 E_3 \alpha_3 \Delta t = 600 \times 200 \times 10^3 \times 11.7 \times 10^{-6} \times 80$

$$= 600 \times 200000 \times 9.36 \times 10^{-4} = 600 \times 187.2 = 112{,}320 \text{ N}$$
$$\{f_t^3\} = 112{,}320 \begin{Bmatrix} -1 \\ 1 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} -112{,}320 \\ 112{,}320 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ N} \quad \text{(nodes 3, 4)}$$

Step 2: Assemble the global temperature load vector by adding element contributions at shared nodes:

$$\{f_t\} = \begin{Bmatrix} f_{t1} \\ f_{t2} \\ f_{t3} \\ f_{t4} \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} -301{,}190.4 \\ 301{,}190.4 + (-154{,}560) \\ 154{,}560 + (-112{,}320) \\ 112{,}320 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} -301{,}190.40 \\ 146{,}630.40 \\ 42{,}240 \\ 112{,}320 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ N}$$

Step 3: External force vector (forces at nodes 2 & 3 are leftward, i.e., negative):

$$\{f_{\text{ext}}\} = \begin{Bmatrix} R_1 \\ -60{,}000 \\ -75{,}000 \\ R_4 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ N}$$

Step 4: Global force vector = External + Temperature:

$$\{F_{\text{global}}\} = \{f_{\text{ext}}\} + \{f_t\} = \begin{Bmatrix} R_1 - 301{,}190.40 \\ -60{,}000 + 146{,}630.40 \\ -75{,}000 + 42{,}240 \\ R_4 + 112{,}320 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} R_1 - 301{,}190.40 \\ 86{,}630.40 \\ -32{,}760 \\ R_4 + 112{,}320 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ N}$$

Displacement vector: $\{u\} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0 \\ u_2 \\ u_3 \\ 0 \end{Bmatrix}$

Step 5: Element stiffness matrices.

Using $k_i = \frac{A_i E_i}{L_i}$:

$$k_1 = \frac{2400 \times 83 \times 10^3}{800} = \frac{199.2 \times 10^6}{800} = 2.49 \times 10^5 \text{ N/mm}$$
$$k_2 = \frac{1200 \times 70 \times 10^3}{600} = \frac{84 \times 10^6}{600} = 1.4 \times 10^5 \text{ N/mm}$$
$$k_3 = \frac{600 \times 200 \times 10^3}{400} = \frac{120 \times 10^6}{400} = 3.0 \times 10^5 \text{ N/mm}$$
$$[K^1] = 10^5 \begin{bmatrix} 2.49 & -2.49 \\ -2.49 & 2.49 \end{bmatrix}, \quad [K^2] = 10^5 \begin{bmatrix} 1.4 & -1.4 \\ -1.4 & 1.4 \end{bmatrix}, \quad [K^3] = 10^5 \begin{bmatrix} 3 & -3 \\ -3 & 3 \end{bmatrix}$$

Step 6: Global stiffness matrix (assembling):

$$[K] = 10^5 \begin{bmatrix} 2.49 & -2.49 & 0 & 0 \\ -2.49 & (2.49+1.4) & -1.4 & 0 \\ 0 & -1.4 & (1.4+3) & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & -3 & 3 \end{bmatrix} = 10^5 \begin{bmatrix} 2.49 & -2.49 & 0 & 0 \\ -2.49 & 3.89 & -1.4 & 0 \\ 0 & -1.4 & 4.4 & -3 \\ 0 & 0 & -3 & 3 \end{bmatrix}$$

Step 7: Apply boundary conditions and solve.

$\{F\} = [K]\{u\}$. Eliminating rows/columns for $u_1 = 0$ and $u_4 = 0$:

$$\begin{Bmatrix} 86{,}630.40 \\ -32{,}760 \end{Bmatrix} = 10^5 \begin{bmatrix} 3.89 & -1.4 \\ -1.4 & 4.4 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix}$$

Using matrix inversion: $\det = 10^{10}(3.89 \times 4.4 - 1.4 \times 1.4) = 10^{10}(17.116 - 1.96) = 15.156 \times 10^{10}$

$$\begin{Bmatrix} u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix} = \frac{1}{15.156 \times 10^{10}} \begin{bmatrix} 4.4 \times 10^5 & 1.4 \times 10^5 \\ 1.4 \times 10^5 & 3.89 \times 10^5 \end{bmatrix} \begin{Bmatrix} 86{,}630.40 \\ -32{,}760 \end{Bmatrix}$$
$$u_2 = \frac{4.4 \times 10^5 \times 86{,}630.40 + 1.4 \times 10^5 \times (-32{,}760)}{15.156 \times 10^{10}} = \frac{38{,}117{,}376 - 4{,}586{,}400}{15.156 \times 10^{10}} = \frac{33{,}530{,}976}{15.156 \times 10^{10}}$$
$$\begin{Bmatrix} u_2 \\ u_3 \end{Bmatrix} = \begin{Bmatrix} 0.22124 \\ -0.00406 \end{Bmatrix} \text{ mm}$$

Step 8: Support reactions.

Row 1 of the full equation: $F_1 = 10^5(2.49 \times 0 - 2.49 \times 0.22124) = 10^5 \times (-0.55089)$

$$F_1 = R_1 - 301{,}190.40 = -55{,}089 \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_1 = -55{,}089 + 301{,}190.40 = 246{,}101.40 \text{ N}$$

Row 4: $F_4 = 10^5(-3 \times (-0.00406) + 3 \times 0) = 10^5 \times 0.01218$

$$F_4 = R_4 + 112{,}320 = 1{,}218 \quad \Rightarrow \quad R_4 = 1{,}218 - 112{,}320 = -111{,}102 \text{ N}$$
$$R_1 = 246{,}101.40 \text{ N} \approx 246.1 \text{ kN}$$ $$R_4 = -111{,}102 \text{ N} \approx -111.1 \text{ kN}$$

Equilibrium check: $R_1 + (-60{,}000) + (-75{,}000) + R_4 = 246{,}101.4 - 60{,}000 - 75{,}000 - 111{,}102 \approx 0$   $\checkmark$

Step 9: Element stresses (with temperature effect).

Using $\sigma = E\left[\frac{1}{L}(u_j - u_i)\right] - E\alpha\Delta t$:

Element 1 ($u_1 = 0$, $u_2 = 0.22124$):

$$\sigma_1 = 83 \times 10^3 \left\{ \frac{0.22124 - 0}{800} - 18.9 \times 10^{-6} \times 80 \right\} = 83000 \left\{ 2.7655 \times 10^{-4} - 1.512 \times 10^{-3} \right\}$$
$$= 83000 \times (-1.2355 \times 10^{-3}) = -102.54 \text{ N/mm}^2 \text{ (Compressive)}$$

Element 2 ($u_2 = 0.22124$, $u_3 = -0.00406$):

$$\sigma_2 = 70 \times 10^3 \left\{ \frac{-0.00406 - 0.22124}{600} - 23 \times 10^{-6} \times 80 \right\} = 70000 \left\{ \frac{-0.2253}{600} - 1.84 \times 10^{-3} \right\}$$
$$= 70000 \times (-3.755 \times 10^{-4} - 1.84 \times 10^{-3}) = 70000 \times (-2.2155 \times 10^{-3}) = -155.09 \text{ N/mm}^2 \text{ (Compressive)}$$

Element 3 ($u_3 = -0.00406$, $u_4 = 0$):

$$\sigma_3 = 200 \times 10^3 \left\{ \frac{0 - (-0.00406)}{400} - 11.7 \times 10^{-6} \times 80 \right\} = 200000 \left\{ 1.015 \times 10^{-5} - 9.36 \times 10^{-4} \right\}$$
$$= 200000 \times (-9.259 \times 10^{-4}) = -185.17 \text{ N/mm}^2 \text{ (Compressive)}$$
$$\sigma_1 = -102.54 \text{ N/mm}^2, \quad \sigma_2 = -155.09 \text{ N/mm}^2, \quad \sigma_3 = -185.17 \text{ N/mm}^2$$

All members are in compression because the temperature expansion is restrained by the fixed supports.

Fig: Tapered bar hanging under self-weight with point load
Note: If area varies linearly, $[K] = \frac{E\bar{A}}{l}\begin{bmatrix} 1 & -1 \\ -1 & 1 \end{bmatrix}$ where $\bar{A} = \frac{A_1 + A_2}{2}$.
